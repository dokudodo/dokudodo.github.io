Are you planning to learn Malayalam and looking for the daily routine word meaning in Malayalam? Then you are in the right place. In this article, we will discuss the meaning of daily routine words in Malayalam.

What is Malayalam?

Malayalam is a language spoken in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is one of the 22 scheduled languages of India and has a rich literary history. Malayalam is the official language of Kerala and Lakshadweep, and is also spoken in other parts of India and abroad.

Why is it important to learn daily routine words in Malayalam?

Learning daily routine words in Malayalam is important if you are planning to visit Kerala or if you want to communicate with Malayalam speakers. By learning these words, you will be able to understand and communicate better with the locals. It will also help you to get around and perform daily tasks with ease.

Daily Routine Word Meaning in Malayalam

1. Wake up – എഴുന്നേറ്റം ചെയ്യുക (Ezhunnertam cheyyuka)

Wake up means to stop sleeping and start the day. In Malayalam, it is എഴുന്നേറ്റം ചെയ്യുക (Ezhunnertam cheyyuka).

2. Brush – പരിചയം ചെയ്യുക (Parichayam cheyyuka)

Brushing your teeth is an important daily routine. In Malayalam, it is പരിചയം ചെയ്യുക (Parichayam cheyyuka).

3. Bath – സ്നാനം ചെയ്യുക (Snaanam cheyyuka)

Bathing is an important part of personal hygiene. In Malayalam, it is സ്നാനം ചെയ്യുക (Snaanam cheyyuka).

4. Breakfast – പലഹാരം (Palahaaram)

Breakfast is the first meal of the day. In Malayalam, it is പലഹാരം (Palahaaram).

5. Lunch – ഉച്ചിപ്പുഴയും (Uchippuzhayum)

Lunch is the midday meal. In Malayalam, it is ഉച്ചിപ്പുഴയും (Uchippuzhayum).

6. Dinner – രാത്രിഭക്ഷണം (Raathribhakshanam)

Dinner is the meal eaten in the evening. In Malayalam, it is രാത്രിഭക്ഷണം (Raathribhakshanam).

7. Sleep – ഉറക്കം ചെയ്യുക (Urakkam cheyyuka)

Sleeping is an important part of our daily routine. In Malayalam, it is ഉറക്കം ചെയ്യുക (Urakkam cheyyuka).

Conclusion

Learning daily routine words in Malayalam is essential if you want to communicate with Malayalam speakers or if you are planning to visit Kerala. By learning these words, you will be able to understand and communicate better with the locals. It will also help you to get around and perform daily tasks with ease.
