Are you a pregnant woman who is struggling to gain weight? It can be tough to know what to eat to put on the pounds during pregnancy, especially if you are experiencing morning sickness or other pregnancy-related symptoms. However, gaining weight during pregnancy is important for the health of both you and your baby.

Protein

Protein is essential for building and repairing tissues, and it is especially important during pregnancy when your body is working hard to create a healthy baby. Some good sources of protein include:

  • Lean meats, such as chicken, turkey, and fish
  • Eggs
  • Dairy products, such as milk, cheese, and yogurt
  • Legumes, such as beans, lentils, and chickpeas
  • Nuts and seeds

Try to incorporate protein into every meal and snack. For example, you could have scrambled eggs and whole wheat toast for breakfast, a turkey sandwich on whole grain bread for lunch, and grilled fish with roasted vegetables for dinner.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates provide energy for your body and are an important part of a healthy diet during pregnancy. However, it is important to choose complex carbohydrates, such as whole grains, fruits, and vegetables, rather than simple carbohydrates, such as sugary snacks and white bread. Some good sources of complex carbohydrates include:

  • Whole grain bread, pasta, and rice
  • Fruits, such as berries, apples, and bananas
  • Vegetables, such as broccoli, spinach, and sweet potatoes

Try to avoid processed foods, which are often high in sugar and unhealthy fats.

Healthy Fats

Fats are important for the development of your baby's brain and nervous system, and they can also help you feel fuller for longer periods of time. However, it is important to choose healthy fats, such as those found in:

  • Avocado
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Fatty fish, such as salmon and tuna
  • Olive oil

Try to avoid saturated and trans fats, which are found in fried foods, processed snacks, and fatty meats.

Fruits and Vegetables

Fruits and vegetables are an important part of a healthy diet during pregnancy because they provide important vitamins and minerals. Some good options include:

  • Leafy greens, such as spinach and kale
  • Berries, such as blueberries and raspberries
  • Citrus fruits, such as oranges and grapefruits
  • Mangoes and papayas

Try to eat a variety of fruits and vegetables to get a range of nutrients.

Tips for Gaining Weight During Pregnancy

If you are struggling to gain weight during pregnancy, here are some tips to help:

  • Eat frequent, small meals throughout the day
  • Choose nutrient-dense foods, such as those listed above
  • Drink milk or a milk alternative with meals
  • Add healthy fats, such as avocado or nuts, to your meals
  • Talk to your doctor about a safe exercise plan that can help you build muscle

FAQs

What if I am experiencing morning sickness?

If you are experiencing morning sickness, it can be tough to eat enough to gain weight. However, try to eat small, frequent meals throughout the day, and stick to bland foods that are easy on your stomach. You may also want to talk to your doctor about medication or other strategies to manage your symptoms.

What if I am a vegetarian or vegan?

If you are a vegetarian or vegan, it is still possible to get all of the nutrients you need to gain weight during pregnancy. Focus on plant-based sources of protein, such as beans, lentils, and tofu, and make sure to eat a variety of fruits and vegetables.

What if I am gaining too much weight during pregnancy?

Gaining weight during pregnancy is important, but gaining too much weight can increase your risk of complications, such as gestational diabetes and high blood pressure. Talk to your doctor about a healthy weight gain plan that is right for you.

The Bottom Line

Gaining weight during pregnancy is important for the health of both you and your baby, but it can be tough to know what to eat to put on the pounds. Focus on nutrient-dense foods, such as lean proteins, complex carbohydrates, healthy fats, and fruits and vegetables, and talk to your doctor if you are experiencing any difficulties.
