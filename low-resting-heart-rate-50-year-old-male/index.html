Description

Resting heart rate (RHR) is the number of times your heart beats per minute while you are at rest. A low resting heart rate is generally considered to be below 60 beats per minute (bpm) in adults. While a low resting heart rate is often a sign of good cardiovascular health, it can also be a cause for concern if it is accompanied by other symptoms.

How to Measure Resting Heart Rate

To measure your resting heart rate, sit down and relax for a few minutes. Place your index and middle fingers on your wrist, just below your thumb. You should be able to feel your pulse. Count the number of beats you feel in 15 seconds, and then multiply that number by 4 to get your resting heart rate in beats per minute.

Causes of Low Resting Heart Rate

There are several possible causes of low resting heart rate in 50 year old males. These include:

  • Regular exercise or physical activity
  • Being in good cardiovascular health
  • Some medications, such as beta-blockers
  • Low thyroid function
  • Athletic training or conditioning
  • Genetics

Symptoms of Low Resting Heart Rate

In most cases, a low resting heart rate is not a cause for concern and does not produce any symptoms. However, if your RHR drops below 50 bpm, you may experience symptoms such as:

  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Fainting or near-fainting spells
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Fatigue or weakness

Treatments for Low Resting Heart Rate

If you are experiencing symptoms of low resting heart rate, it is important to see your healthcare provider. Treatment options may include:

  • Discontinuing medications that may be contributing to the low heart rate
  • Changing the dose of medications that may be contributing to the low heart rate
  • Treating underlying medical conditions, such as low thyroid function
  • Implanting a pacemaker, which is a small device that helps regulate your heart rate

FAQ

Q: Is a low resting heart rate dangerous?

A: In most cases, a low resting heart rate is not dangerous and is actually a sign of good cardiovascular health. However, if you are experiencing symptoms such as dizziness or fainting spells, it is important to see your healthcare provider.

Q: Can regular exercise cause a low resting heart rate?

A: Yes, regular exercise is one of the most common causes of a low resting heart rate. This is generally considered to be a sign of good cardiovascular health.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • A low resting heart rate is often a sign of good cardiovascular health
  • Regular exercise can lead to a lower resting heart rate

Cons:

  • If your RHR drops below 50 bpm, you may experience symptoms such as dizziness or fainting spells
  • If you are experiencing symptoms of low resting heart rate, it is important to see your healthcare provider
