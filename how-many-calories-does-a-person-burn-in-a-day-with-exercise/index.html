How to Calculate Calories Burned

The number of calories you burn during exercise depends on various factors, including your weight, age, and the intensity of the exercise. To calculate the number of calories you burn during a workout, you can use an online calculator or use the following formula:

Calories Burned = (METs x Weight in Kg x Time in hours)

METs (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) is a measure of the energy expended during physical activity. One MET is equal to the energy expended while sitting quietly. The METs for different exercises can be found online or in fitness books.

For example, if you weigh 70 kg and run for 30 minutes at a speed of 8 km/h, the MET value for running is 8.3 METs. Using the formula above, the number of calories burned during this workout would be:

Calories Burned = (8.3 x 70 x 0.5) = 290.5 calories

Types of Exercises and Their Calorie Burn

Here are some examples of exercises and the approximate number of calories burned per hour for a person weighing 70 kg:

  • Running at a speed of 8 km/h: 590 calories
  • Biking at a moderate pace: 420 calories
  • Swimming at a moderate pace: 420 calories
  • Walking at a brisk pace: 280 calories
  • Strength training: 280 calories

It's important to note that these numbers are just estimates and may vary depending on various factors such as the individual's weight, age, and fitness level.

Tips for Burning More Calories

If you want to burn more calories during exercise, here are some tips:

  • Increase the intensity of your workout
  • Try high-intensity interval training (HIIT)
  • Include strength training in your workout routine
  • Exercise for longer periods of time
  • Try new types of exercises to challenge your body

Pros and Cons of Burning Calories through Exercise

There are many benefits to burning calories through exercise, including weight loss, improved cardiovascular health, and increased energy levels. However, there are also some downsides to consider:

Pros

  • Weight loss
  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Increased energy levels
  • Reduced risk of chronic diseases

Cons

  • Can be time-consuming
  • May cause injury if done improperly
  • May require expensive equipment or gym memberships
  • May not be sustainable for some people

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories should I burn in a day?

The number of calories you should burn in a day depends on various factors such as your age, weight, and activity level. However, a general guideline is to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week.

Can I burn calories without exercise?

Yes, you can burn calories without exercise by increasing your daily physical activity, such as taking the stairs instead of the elevator or doing household chores.

Can I lose weight by only exercising?

While exercise is important for weight loss, it's not the only factor. A healthy diet is also crucial for weight loss. It's important to create a calorie deficit by burning calories through exercise and consuming fewer calories through a healthy diet.

How often should I exercise?

It's recommended to exercise at least 3-4 times per week for optimal health benefits. However, any amount of physical activity is better than none.


In conclusion, the number of calories you burn during exercise depends on various factors, and it's important to find a workout routine that works for you. By incorporating exercise into your daily routine and making healthy choices, you can achieve your fitness goals and maintain a healthy lifestyle.
