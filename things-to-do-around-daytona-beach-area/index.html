Are you planning a trip to Daytona Beach in 2023 and wondering what to do in the area? Look no further, as we have compiled a list of the top things to do in and around Daytona Beach. From beaches to museums, there's something for everyone in this Florida city.

Beaches

Daytona Beach is known for its beautiful beaches, and there are plenty to choose from. Whether you're looking for a quiet spot to relax or a place to surf, Daytona Beach has it all. Some of the top beaches in the area include:

Daytona Beach

Located in the heart of the city, Daytona Beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing. The beach is over 23 miles long and offers plenty of space for visitors.

New Smyrna Beach

About 20 minutes south of Daytona Beach, New Smyrna Beach is a quieter option for those looking to escape the crowds. This beach is known for its excellent surfing conditions.

Ponce Inlet Beach

Located at the southern end of the peninsula, Ponce Inlet Beach is a great spot for fishing and surfing. The beach is also home to the Ponce de Leon Inlet Lighthouse, which offers tours to visitors.

Museums

If you're interested in history or art, Daytona Beach has several museums worth visiting.

Halifax Historical Museum

Located in downtown Daytona Beach, the Halifax Historical Museum offers exhibits on the history of the area, including its role in the Civil War and the racing industry.

Museum of Arts and Sciences

This museum features a collection of art, science, and history exhibits, including a planetarium and a giant screen theater.

Parks

Daytona Beach is home to several parks, perfect for picnics, hiking, and other outdoor activities.

Tomoka State Park

Located about 20 minutes north of Daytona Beach, Tomoka State Park offers hiking trails, fishing, and camping. The park is also home to a historic site, the ruins of an 18th-century plantation.

Tuscawilla Park

This park is located in the heart of Daytona Beach and features a playground, picnic areas, and a pond for fishing.

Shopping

If you're looking for some retail therapy, Daytona Beach has several shopping options.

Volusia Mall

This mall features over 120 stores, including Macy's, JCPenney, and Dillard's. There's also a food court and a movie theater.

Ocean Walk Shoppes

This outdoor shopping and entertainment complex is located on the beach and features stores, restaurants, and a movie theater.

Restaurants

Daytona Beach has a variety of restaurants, offering everything from seafood to pizza.

Ocean Deck Restaurant and Beach Club

This beachfront restaurant is known for its seafood and live music. It's a great spot to grab a drink and watch the sunset.

Steve's Famous Diner

This classic diner serves up breakfast all day, as well as burgers, sandwiches, and milkshakes.

Nightlife

Looking to let loose after a long day of exploring? Daytona Beach has several options for nightlife.

Boot Hill Saloon

This biker bar has been a Daytona Beach staple for over 30 years. It's known for its live music and cold beer.

Razzle's Nightclub

This nightclub features multiple dance floors and a variety of music, from hip-hop to country.

Sports

Daytona Beach is home to several sports teams and hosts several events throughout the year.

Daytona International Speedway

This iconic racetrack is home to the Daytona 500 and other NASCAR events. Visitors can take a tour of the track and even drive on it themselves.

Daytona Tortugas

This minor league baseball team plays at Jackie Robinson Ballpark in downtown Daytona Beach.

Attractions

In addition to beaches and museums, Daytona Beach has several unique attractions.

Daytona Beach Boardwalk and Pier

This oceanfront boardwalk features rides, games, and food vendors. It's a great spot for families.

Angell and Phelps Chocolate Factory

This chocolate factory offers tours and tastings, as well as a gift shop.

Events

Daytona Beach hosts several events throughout the year, including festivals and races.

Daytona Beach Bike Week

This annual event takes place over 10 days in March and features motorcycle races, concerts, and other events.

Halifax Art Festival

This two-day festival takes place in November and features over 250 artists, as well as live music and food vendors.

Day Trips

If you have some extra time, there are several day trips you can take from Daytona Beach.

St. Augustine

About an hour and a half north of Daytona Beach, St. Augustine is the oldest city in the United States. It's known for its historic sites, including the Castillo de San Marcos.

Kennedy Space Center

About an hour and a half south of Daytona Beach, the Kennedy Space Center offers tours and exhibits on the history of space exploration.

With so much to see and do, Daytona Beach is the perfect destination for your next vacation. Whether you're interested in history, outdoor activities, or just lounging on the beach, this Florida city has something for everyone.
