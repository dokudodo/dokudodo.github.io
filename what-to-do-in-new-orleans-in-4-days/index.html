What To Do In New Orleans In 4 Days A Relaxing Guide

If you're planning a trip to New Orleans for four days, you're in for a treat. This city is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and amazing food. In this guide, we'll show you how to make the most of your time in New Orleans, including what to do, where to eat, and where to stay. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Day 1: French Quarter and Bourbon Street
  • Day 2: Garden District and City Park
  • Day 3: Jazz and Blues Music Scene
  • Day 4: Day Trip to Plantations and Swamps

Day 1: French Quarter and Bourbon Street

Your first day in New Orleans should be spent exploring the iconic French Quarter and Bourbon Street. Start by grabbing breakfast at Cafe Du Monde, where you can indulge in their famous beignets and coffee with chicory. After breakfast, take a stroll through Jackson Square and admire the stunning St. Louis Cathedral.

Next, head to Bourbon Street and take in the lively atmosphere. You'll find plenty of bars, restaurants, and shops to explore. Don't miss the chance to try a Hurricane cocktail at Pat O'Brien's, a New Orleans classic.

In the evening, head back to the French Quarter and enjoy a dinner of classic Creole cuisine at one of the many restaurants in the area.

Day 2: Garden District and City Park

On your second day in New Orleans, explore the charming Garden District. Start by taking a guided walking tour to learn about the history and architecture of this beautiful area. After the tour, be sure to stop by Lafayette Cemetery No. 1, one of the oldest cemeteries in New Orleans.

In the afternoon, head to City Park and enjoy a leisurely stroll through the lush greenery. Don't miss the chance to visit the New Orleans Museum of Art, which features an impressive collection of art from around the world.

In the evening, head to Magazine Street and explore the many shops, galleries, and restaurants in the area.

Day 3: Jazz and Blues Music Scene

No trip to New Orleans is complete without experiencing the city's vibrant music scene. Start your day by visiting the historic Preservation Hall, a music venue that has been showcasing jazz music since the 1960s.

In the afternoon, head to Frenchmen Street and explore the many bars and music venues in the area. Don't miss the chance to see a live performance at the famous Blue Nile, which features a variety of jazz and blues musicians.

In the evening, head to the Spotted Cat Music Club and enjoy live music and drinks in a cozy setting.

Day 4: Day Trip to Plantations and Swamps

On your final day in New Orleans, take a day trip to explore the beautiful plantations and swamps outside the city. Start by visiting the famous Oak Alley Plantation, which features stunning antebellum architecture and beautiful gardens.

After the plantation, head to the nearby swamps and take a guided airboat tour. You'll have the chance to see alligators, birds, and other wildlife in their natural habitat.

In the evening, head back to New Orleans and enjoy a farewell dinner at one of the city's many amazing restaurants.

Tips for Your Trip

  • Be sure to try the local cuisine, including gumbo, jambalaya, and po'boys.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for all the walking you'll be doing.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.
  • Be respectful of the city's history and culture.
  • Book tours and activities in advance to avoid lines and crowds.

Conclusion

New Orleans is a city that truly has something for everyone. From the historic French Quarter to the vibrant music scene, there's no shortage of things to do and see. With this guide, you'll be able to make the most of your four days in New Orleans and experience all the city has to offer.
