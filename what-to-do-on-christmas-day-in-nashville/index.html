Christmas is a wonderful time of the year, full of joy, love, and happiness. If you're spending Christmas Day in Nashville, you're in for a treat! Nashville is a vibrant city, filled with exciting activities that are perfect for the whole family. In this article, we'll take a look at what to do on Christmas Day in Nashville, providing you with a range of activities to choose from.

1. Visit the Christmas Lights

If you're looking for a fun and festive activity to do on Christmas Day in Nashville, then visiting the Christmas lights is a must. You can take a stroll through the city's many neighborhoods and admire the beautiful light displays put up by residents. Some popular spots to visit include Cheekwood Estate & Gardens, Opryland Resort, and the Gaylord Opryland Resort & Convention Center.

Pros:

  • Free activity
  • Great for all ages
  • Easy to do

Cons:

  • Cold weather
  • Traffic may be heavy

2. Go Ice Skating

If you're looking for a fun and active activity to do on Christmas Day in Nashville, then ice skating is a great option. The Ford Ice Center, located in Antioch and Bellevue, offers public skating sessions throughout the day. The rinks are beautifully decorated for the holidays, making it a magical experience for everyone.

Pros:

  • Fun and active activity
  • Great for all ages
  • Good exercise

Cons:

  • May be crowded
  • Costs money
  • Requires some skill

3. Attend a Christmas Concert

If you're a music lover, then attending a Christmas concert is a great way to spend Christmas Day in Nashville. Many venues, such as the Ryman Auditorium and the Schermerhorn Symphony Center, offer special holiday concerts featuring local artists and musicians.

Pros:

  • Great for music lovers
  • Festive atmosphere
  • Good entertainment

Cons:

  • Costs money
  • May be sold out
  • Not suitable for all ages

4. Watch Christmas Movies

If you're looking for a cozy and relaxing activity to do on Christmas Day in Nashville, then watching Christmas movies is a great option. Many streaming services, such as Netflix and Hulu, offer a wide selection of holiday movies to choose from. You can also rent movies from local video stores, such as The Great Escape.

Pros:

  • Cozy and relaxing activity
  • Good for all ages
  • Indoor activity

Cons:

  • May be boring for some
  • Requires a TV or device
  • May be distracting if done with a large group

5. Have a Christmas Dinner

Having a Christmas dinner is a great way to spend Christmas Day in Nashville with family and friends. Many restaurants offer special holiday menus, such as the Loveless Cafe and Puckett's Gro. You can also cook your own meal at home, using traditional recipes and ingredients.

Pros:

  • Great for family and friends
  • Good food
  • Festive atmosphere

Cons:

  • Costs money
  • May require reservations
  • May be crowded

6. Go Shopping

If you're looking to do some last-minute shopping, then Christmas Day in Nashville is a great time to do it. Many stores, such as Opry Mills and Green Hills Mall, offer special holiday hours and deals. You can also visit local boutiques and shops, such as White's Mercantile and Imogene + Willie.

Pros:

  • Good for last-minute shopping
  • Great deals
  • Good for all ages

Cons:

  • May be crowded
  • Costs money
  • May be exhausting

7. Visit a Museum

If you're interested in history and culture, then visiting a museum is a great way to spend Christmas Day in Nashville. The Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum, the Frist Art Museum, and the Tennessee State Museum are all great options.

Pros:

  • Great for history and culture lovers
  • Indoor activity
  • Good for all ages

Cons:

  • Costs money
  • May be crowded
  • May be boring for some

8. Go on a Horse Carriage Ride

If you're looking for a romantic and picturesque activity to do on Christmas Day in Nashville, then going on a horse carriage ride is a great option. Many companies, such as Nashville Horse & Carriage and Sugar Creek Carriages, offer special holiday rides throughout the city.

Pros:

  • Romantic and picturesque activity
  • Festive atmosphere
  • Good for all ages

Cons:

  • Costs money
  • May be cold
  • May not be suitable for large groups

9. Attend a Christmas Service

If you're religious, then attending a Christmas service is a great way to spend Christmas Day in Nashville. Many churches, such as the Cathedral of the Incarnation and the First Baptist Church, offer special holiday services throughout the day.

Pros:

  • Great for religious individuals
  • Festive atmosphere
  • Good for all ages

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for non-religious individuals
  • May be crowded
  • May require dress code

10. Go on a Christmas Tour

If you're interested in learning more about Nashville's history and culture, then going on a Christmas tour is a great way to spend Christmas Day in Nashville. Many companies, such as Old Town Trolley Tours and NashTrash Tours, offer special holiday tours throughout the city.

Pros:

  • Great for history and culture lovers
  • Good for all ages
  • Festive atmosphere

Cons:

  • Costs money
