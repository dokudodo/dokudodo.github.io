Fun Day Things To Do In London

Written by Bowie Jul 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

.

London is a city that offers a variety of fun day activities for locals and tourists alike. Whether you're interested in exploring the city's rich history, indulging in delicious food or simply soaking up the atmosphere, there's something for everyone. In this article, we'll explore some of the best fun day things to do in London, along with tips and tricks to make the most of your day.

Visit the British Museum

The British Museum is one of the most popular attractions in London, and for good reason. The museum houses a vast collection of artifacts from around the world, including the Rosetta Stone and the Parthenon sculptures. Admission to the museum is free, although some exhibitions may require a fee. It's recommended to arrive early to avoid crowds and make the most of your visit.

Tips:

  • Download the museum's app for a self-guided tour
  • Check the museum's website for special exhibitions and events
  • Leave plenty of time to explore the museum

Take a Stroll in Hyde Park

Hyde Park is a beautiful green space in the heart of London, perfect for a relaxing day out. The park features several walking and cycling paths, as well as a boating lake and a playground. During the summer months, the park hosts various events and concerts, making it a great place to soak up some culture.

Tips:

  • Pack a picnic and enjoy lunch in the park
  • Visit the Serpentine Gallery for contemporary art exhibitions
  • Take a horse riding lesson at the Hyde Park Stables

Explore the Tower of London

The Tower of London is a historic castle that has served as a royal palace, prison, and fortress throughout the centuries. The castle is home to the Crown Jewels, as well as several exhibitions detailing the castle's history. Guided tours are available, but it's also possible to explore the castle on your own.

Tips:

  • Buy tickets online in advance to avoid queues
  • Plan to spend at least 2-3 hours exploring the castle
  • Visit the White Tower for a glimpse into medieval life

Indulge in Street Food at Borough Market

Borough Market is a food lover's paradise, with stalls selling everything from fresh produce to artisanal cheeses. The market is open from Monday to Saturday, and it's recommended to arrive early to avoid crowds. Some stalls also offer free samples, so don't be afraid to try something new.

Tips:

  • Bring cash as some stalls don't accept card payments
  • Try the famous grilled cheese sandwich from Kappacasein
  • Visit on a Friday or Saturday for the full market experience

Shop at Oxford Street

Oxford Street is one of the busiest shopping streets in Europe, with over 300 shops catering to all budgets and tastes. From high-end fashion to high-street brands, there's something for everyone. The street is also decorated with festive lights during the holiday season, making it a magical place to shop.

Tips:

  • Visit early in the morning or late in the evening to avoid crowds
  • Check out Selfridges for luxury shopping
  • Stop by Primark for affordable fashion finds

Take a Thames River Cruise

A Thames River Cruise is a great way to see London from a different perspective. Several companies offer cruises of varying lengths and prices, with some even including food and drink. Cruises usually depart from Westminster or Tower Bridge, and provide stunning views of London's iconic landmarks.

Tips:

  • Book in advance to secure your preferred cruise time
  • Bring a camera to capture the views
  • Opt for a sunset cruise for a romantic experience

Discover Art at Tate Modern

Tate Modern is a contemporary art museum located in a former power station on the banks of the Thames. The museum houses works by some of the world's most famous artists, including Picasso, Warhol and Hockney. Admission to the museum is free, although some exhibitions may require a fee.

Tips:

  • Visit the museum's rooftop terrace for panoramic views of London
  • Check out the museum's calendar for special events and exhibitions
  • Grab a coffee at the museum's cafe for a break from exploring

Watch a Show at West End

West End is London's theater district, with several theaters hosting a variety of shows every night. From musicals to plays, there's something for every taste. It's recommended to book tickets in advance, especially for popular shows.

Tips:

  • Check out discount ticket websites for deals on last-minute tickets
  • Arrive early to grab a drink at the theater bar
  • Dress up for a special night out

Visit the London Eye

The London Eye is a giant Ferris wheel located on the South Bank of the Thames. The wheel provides stunning views of London's skyline, with each capsule holding up to 25 people. It's recommended to book tickets in advance to avoid queues.

Tips:

  • Visit at sunset for a romantic experience
  • Upgrade to a private capsule for a VIP experience
  • Take a souvenir photo at the end of the ride

Take a Harry Potter Tour

For fans of the Harry Potter series, a tour of the city's filming locations is a must-do. Several companies offer guided tours, taking visitors to spots such as Platform 9 3/4 and Diagon Alley. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak season.

Tips:

  • Bring a camera to capture the magic
  • Dress up in your Hogwarts robes for a fun experience
  • Stop by the Harry Potter Studio Tour for an even deeper dive into the series
In conclusion, London offers a wide variety of fun day activities for every taste and budget. From exploring the city's rich history to indulging in delicious food, there's something for everyone. With a little planning and some insider tips, you can make the most of your day in London and create memories that will last a lifetime.
