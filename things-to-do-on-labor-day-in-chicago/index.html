Things To Do On Labor Day In Chicago

Written by April Mar 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Labor Day celebrated on the first Monday of September every year is a day dedicated to the contributions made by American workers It also marks the end of summer and the beginning of fall If you re in Chicago for Labor Day there are plenty of things to do to celebrate the occasion In this article we ll be discussing some of the best things you can do on Labor Day in Chicago .

Labor Day, celebrated on the first Monday of September every year, is a day dedicated to the contributions made by American workers. It also marks the end of summer and the beginning of fall. If you’re in Chicago for Labor Day, there are plenty of things to do to celebrate the occasion. In this article, we’ll be discussing some of the best things you can do on Labor Day in Chicago.

1. Attend the Chicago Jazz Festival

The Chicago Jazz Festival is one of the most popular events in the city and takes place every year over Labor Day weekend. The festival features performances by renowned jazz musicians from around the world and is completely free to attend. You can enjoy great music, food, and drinks while soaking up the vibrant atmosphere of the city.

2. Take a River Cruise

Chicago is famous for its architecture, and one of the best ways to see it is by taking a river cruise. On Labor Day, several cruise companies offer special tours that allow you to see the city from a unique perspective. You can learn about the history and architecture of some of the city’s most famous buildings while enjoying the beautiful scenery.

3. Visit the Navy Pier

The Navy Pier is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Chicago and is a great place to spend Labor Day. The pier offers a variety of activities, such as the Chicago Children’s Museum, the Chicago Shakespeare Theater, and the Chicago IMAX Theater. You can also enjoy delicious food and drinks at one of the many restaurants or bars on the pier.

4. Attend the Great American Lobster Fest

The Great American Lobster Fest is a must-attend event for seafood lovers. The festival takes place on Labor Day weekend and features fresh lobster flown in from Maine, as well as other seafood dishes, live music, and family-friendly activities.

5. Go to the Beach

Chicago has some of the best beaches in the country, and Labor Day is the perfect time to enjoy them. You can soak up the sun, swim in the lake, and have a picnic with family and friends. Some of the best beaches to visit on Labor Day are North Avenue Beach, Oak Street Beach, and Montrose Beach.

6. Check out the Chicago Cultural Center

The Chicago Cultural Center is a beautiful building that serves as a hub for the arts in the city. On Labor Day, the center offers a variety of events and activities, such as art exhibits, live music, and dance performances. You can also take a free guided tour of the building and learn about its history and architecture.

7. Attend the African Festival of the Arts

The African Festival of the Arts is a four-day event that takes place over Labor Day weekend. The festival celebrates African culture and features live music, dance performances, art exhibits, and food vendors. You can also shop for unique African crafts and souvenirs at the festival.

8. Visit the Lincoln Park Zoo

The Lincoln Park Zoo is a great place to spend the day with family and friends. The zoo is home to over 1,000 animals from around the world and is completely free to visit. On Labor Day, the zoo offers special events and activities, such as animal feedings and educational programs.

9. Attend a Cubs Game

If you’re a baseball fan, attending a Cubs game is a must-do on Labor Day. The Cubs play at Wrigley Field, one of the most iconic ballparks in the country. You can enjoy a hot dog and a beer while cheering on the home team.

10. Take a Bike Tour

Biking is a great way to explore the city, and several companies offer bike tours of Chicago. On Labor Day, you can take a special tour that highlights the city’s history and architecture. You can also choose to rent a bike and explore the city on your own.

Conclusion

Chicago is a vibrant city with plenty of things to do on Labor Day. Whether you’re interested in music, history, or just relaxing on the beach, there’s something for everyone. So why not plan a trip to Chicago this Labor Day and enjoy all that the city has to offer?
