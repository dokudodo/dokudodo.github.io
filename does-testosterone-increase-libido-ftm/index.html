What is FTM?

FTM stands for female-to-male, and it refers to individuals who were assigned female at birth but identify as male. FTM individuals may undergo various forms of gender-affirming medical interventions to align their physical appearance with their gender identity. One of these interventions is testosterone supplementation.

What is testosterone?

Testosterone is a hormone that is primarily produced in the testicles in males, and in smaller amounts in the ovaries and adrenal glands in females. It is responsible for various male characteristics, such as muscle mass, bone density, body hair, and voice deepening. Testosterone also plays a key role in sexual function, including libido and erectile function.

How does testosterone affect libido?

Testosterone has a direct impact on the libido of both males and females. In males, testosterone is the primary hormone responsible for sex drive, and its levels can affect the frequency and intensity of sexual thoughts and desires. In females, testosterone is also important for sexual function and can increase libido when supplemented in appropriate amounts.

How is testosterone supplemented?

Testosterone can be supplemented in various forms, including injections, gels, patches, and pellets. The most common form of testosterone supplementation for FTM individuals is intramuscular injections, which are typically given weekly or biweekly. The dosage and frequency of testosterone supplementation will depend on various factors, such as age, weight, and individual response.

Pros and cons of testosterone supplementation for FTM individuals

Testosterone supplementation can have various benefits for FTM individuals, including:

  • Increased muscle mass and strength
  • Deepening of the voice
  • Growth of facial and body hair
  • Alleviation of gender dysphoria
  • Improved self-esteem and confidence

However, testosterone supplementation also carries some risks and side effects, such as:

  • Acne
  • Hair loss
  • Increased risk of heart disease and stroke
  • Decreased fertility
  • Increased risk of blood clots

It is important for FTM individuals to discuss the risks and benefits of testosterone supplementation with their healthcare provider and to undergo regular monitoring of their hormone levels and overall health.

Frequently asked questions

Q: Does testosterone always increase libido in FTM individuals?

A: Testosterone can increase libido in FTM individuals, but the degree of change can vary depending on individual factors such as age, baseline hormone levels, and overall health.

Q: Can testosterone supplementation cause infertility in FTM individuals?

A: Testosterone supplementation can decrease fertility in FTM individuals, but it is not always permanent. FTM individuals who wish to preserve their fertility may consider sperm banking before starting testosterone supplementation.

Q: Is it safe for FTM individuals to self-administer testosterone?

A: No, it is not safe for FTM individuals to self-administer testosterone. Testosterone supplementation should only be administered under the supervision of a qualified healthcare provider who can monitor hormone levels and overall health.

In conclusion, testosterone can increase libido in FTM individuals, but its effects can vary depending on individual factors. Testosterone supplementation carries both benefits and risks, and it is important for FTM individuals to discuss their options with a qualified healthcare provider.
