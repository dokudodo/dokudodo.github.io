Day Trips In Nashville Exploring The Best Of Music City

If you're looking for a fun and exciting day trip in Nashville, you're in for a treat! The city is filled with tons of exciting things to see and do, from historic sites to live music venues and more. Whether you're a local or a tourist, there's always something new to discover in Nashville. Here are some of the best day trips to take in Music City!

How to Get Around Nashville

The best way to get around Nashville is by car, as many of the attractions are spread throughout the city. However, if you prefer not to drive, there are plenty of other transportation options available, including buses, taxis, and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft. You can also rent a bike or take a walking tour of the city if you prefer.

Day Trip #1: Visit the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum

If you're a fan of country music, you won't want to miss the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum. This iconic attraction is located in the heart of downtown Nashville and features exhibits on some of the biggest names in country music, including Johnny Cash, Dolly Parton, and Hank Williams. You can also check out live performances, educational programs, and special events throughout the year.

How to Get There

The Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum is located at 222 5th Ave S, Nashville, TN 37203. You can easily drive there or take a taxi, bus, or ride-sharing service. If you prefer to walk, it's only a few blocks from many of the downtown hotels and attractions.

Day Trip #2: Explore the Belle Meade Plantation

If you're interested in history and architecture, the Belle Meade Plantation is a must-see attraction in Nashville. This beautiful antebellum mansion has been restored to its former glory and offers tours of the grounds and gardens, as well as the mansion itself. You can also sample some of the famous Belle Meade Bourbon and learn about the history of this iconic Tennessee brand.

How to Get There

The Belle Meade Plantation is located at 5025 Harding Pike, Nashville, TN 37205. It's about 8 miles from downtown Nashville, so you'll need to drive or take a taxi or ride-sharing service. There is also limited public transportation available.

Day Trip #3: Visit the Jack Daniel's Distillery

Located about an hour and a half south of Nashville, the Jack Daniel's Distillery is a must-visit attraction for whiskey lovers. You can take a tour of the distillery and see how Jack Daniel's whiskey is made, as well as sample some of the different varieties. There's also a gift shop where you can purchase souvenirs and bottles of Jack Daniel's to take home.

How to Get There

The Jack Daniel's Distillery is located at 133 Lynchburg Hwy, Lynchburg, TN 37352. It's about 70 miles south of Nashville, so you'll need to drive or take a tour bus. There are several tour companies that offer day trips from Nashville to the distillery.

Tips for Enjoying Your Day Trip in Nashville

No matter which day trip you choose in Nashville, there are a few tips to keep in mind to make the most of your experience. First, be sure to dress comfortably, as you'll likely be doing a lot of walking and standing. You'll also want to bring a camera to capture all the amazing sights and sounds of Music City.

Another tip is to plan your day trip in advance, especially if you're visiting during peak tourist season. Many of the attractions can get crowded, so it's a good idea to book tickets and make reservations ahead of time. Finally, be sure to budget your time and money wisely, as there's so much to see and do in Nashville that it can be easy to overspend or run out of time.

FAQs

What are some other day trips to take in Nashville?

Other popular day trips in Nashville include visiting the Grand Ole Opry, exploring the Ryman Auditorium, and taking a stroll down Broadway to see the live music and honky-tonks.

Is it safe to travel to Nashville?

Yes, Nashville is generally a safe and friendly city for tourists. However, as with any city, it's important to stay aware of your surroundings and take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

Pros and Cons of Taking a Day Trip in Nashville

Pros:

  • There are tons of exciting attractions to see and do
  • You can enjoy the best of Music City without having to stay overnight
  • You can customize your itinerary to fit your interests and budget

Cons:

  • Some of the attractions can be crowded, especially during peak tourist season
  • You may need to rent a car or use a ride-sharing service to get around
  • You may not have enough time to see everything you want to see in one day

No matter what your interests are, there's something for everyone in Nashville. So why not plan a day trip and explore the best of Music City today?
