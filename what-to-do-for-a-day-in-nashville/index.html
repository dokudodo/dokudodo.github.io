What To Do For A Day In Nashville A Relaxing Guide

Welcome to Nashville, the Music City! Whether you're visiting for the first time or you're a seasoned traveler, Nashville has something for everyone. In this guide, we'll show you how to spend a relaxing day in Nashville, with plenty of things to see and do. From delicious food to live music, we've got you covered.

  • Museums

1. Breakfast

Start your day in Nashville with a delicious breakfast. One of the most popular spots for breakfast is the Pancake Pantry. This diner has been serving up delicious pancakes for over 60 years. Try their famous sweet potato pancakes or a classic stack with bacon and eggs.

Another great spot for breakfast is Biscuit Love. They serve up some of the best biscuits in town, with a variety of toppings to choose from. Try the East Nasty, a biscuit sandwich with fried chicken, cheddar cheese, and sausage gravy.

2. Museums

Nashville is home to some amazing museums, so take some time to explore them on your day trip. The Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum is a must-visit for music lovers. Learn about the history of country music and see artifacts from some of the biggest stars in the industry.

Another great museum to check out is the Johnny Cash Museum. This museum is dedicated to the life and career of the Man in Black. See his guitars, costumes, and personal belongings on display.

3. Lunch

After a morning of exploring museums, it's time for lunch. Head to Prince's Hot Chicken for some of the best hot chicken in Nashville. This spicy fried chicken is a Nashville classic, and Prince's has been serving it up for over 70 years.

If you're in the mood for something lighter, head to The Pharmacy Burger. This restaurant serves up delicious burgers, fries, and milkshakes. Try the Farm Burger, topped with bacon, cheddar cheese, and a fried egg.

4. Parks

Nashville has some beautiful parks, perfect for a relaxing afternoon. Head to Centennial Park to see the Parthenon, a full-scale replica of the original in Athens. Take a walk around the lake and enjoy the beauty of the park.

If you're looking for something more active, head to Percy Warner Park. This park has miles of hiking trails, perfect for a scenic walk or run. You'll also see some beautiful views of the city.

5. Dinner

For dinner, head to Husk. This restaurant serves up delicious Southern cuisine, with a focus on local ingredients. Try the fried chicken or the shrimp and grits.

Another great option is Rolf and Daughters. This restaurant serves up modern Italian cuisine, with a focus on fresh ingredients. Try the handmade pasta or the wood-fired pizza.

6. Live Music

No trip to Nashville is complete without seeing some live music. Head to the Bluebird Cafe for an intimate experience. This small venue has hosted some of the biggest names in country music, and you never know who might show up for a surprise performance.

If you're looking for something more lively, head to the Honky Tonk Highway. This stretch of bars and clubs is known for its live music and party atmosphere. Check out Tootsie's Orchid Lounge or The Stage for some great music and drinks.

Tips for a Great Day in Nashville

  • Wear comfortable shoes - Nashville is a walking city, and you'll want to be comfortable as you explore
  • Bring sunscreen - Nashville can get hot in the summer, so protect your skin
  • Try the hot chicken - It's a Nashville classic!
  • Be prepared for crowds - Nashville is a popular destination, so be prepared for crowds at popular spots

FAQ

Q: What is Nashville known for?

A: Nashville is known for its music scene, with a focus on country music. It's also known for its delicious food, including hot chicken and biscuits.

Q: What is the best time to visit Nashville?

A: The best time to visit Nashville is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. However, Nashville is a great destination year-round.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Great food
  • Amazing music scene
  • Beautiful parks

Cons:

  • Can be crowded at popular spots
  • Can be hot in the summer

Overall, Nashville is a fantastic destination for a relaxing day trip. From delicious food to beautiful parks, there's something for everyone in the Music City.
