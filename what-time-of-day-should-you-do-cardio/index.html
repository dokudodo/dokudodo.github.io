If you're wondering what time of day you should do cardio, you're not alone. Many people are confused about when the best time to do cardio is, and whether it really matters at all. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of doing cardio at different times of day, and give you some tips on how to decide when to schedule your workouts.

Why Does the Time of Day Matter?

The time of day you do cardio can impact your performance, energy levels, and even your sleep quality. For example, if you do cardio too close to bedtime, it can interfere with your ability to fall asleep. On the other hand, doing cardio in the morning can help you wake up and start your day off on the right foot.

There is also some evidence to suggest that the time of day you do cardio can impact your metabolism and fat-burning potential. Some studies have found that doing cardio in a fasted state (i.e. before breakfast) can increase fat burning, while others have found that doing cardio after a meal can be more effective for weight loss.

Morning Cardio

Many people swear by morning cardio, and for good reason. Doing cardio first thing in the morning can help you wake up, boost your metabolism, and set a positive tone for the rest of your day. It can also be a great way to get your exercise out of the way before you get too busy with work or other responsibilities.

One of the main drawbacks of morning cardio is that it can be tough to get motivated to exercise when you're still feeling groggy from sleep. It can also be challenging to find the time to fit in a workout if you have to get up extra early to do it.

Afternoon Cardio

Doing cardio in the afternoon can be a good option for people who need a midday energy boost or who prefer to exercise after they've had a chance to wake up and get moving. Some studies have also found that afternoon exercise can be more effective for weight loss than morning exercise, as your metabolism is naturally higher later in the day.

The downside of afternoon cardio is that it can be hard to make time for it if you have a busy work schedule or other commitments in the afternoon. It can also be tough to motivate yourself to work out after a long day at the office.

Evening Cardio

Some people prefer to do cardio in the evening, as it can be a good way to wind down after a long day and relieve stress. It can also be a good option if you struggle to wake up early enough to do morning cardio.

The main downside of evening cardio is that it can interfere with your sleep if you do it too close to bedtime. It can also be challenging to find the motivation to work out after a long day, especially if you're feeling tired or stressed.

Tips for Choosing the Best Time for You

So, how do you decide when to do cardio? Here are a few tips:

  • Consider your schedule: Choose a time when you're least likely to be interrupted or distracted by other commitments.
  • Think about your energy levels: Choose a time when you're naturally more alert and awake, whether that's in the morning, afternoon, or evening.
  • Experiment: Try working out at different times of day and see how you feel. You may find that you prefer one time over another.
  • Be consistent: Once you find a time that works for you, stick with it. Consistency is key for building a fitness habit.

FAQs

Does it matter what type of cardio I do?

Not necessarily. The time of day you do cardio is more important than the type of cardio you do. That being said, different types of cardio may be better suited to different times of day. For example, high-intensity interval training (HIIT) may be more effective in the morning, while low-intensity steady-state (LISS) cardio may be better in the afternoon or evening.

How long should I do cardio for?

The amount of cardio you should do depends on your fitness level and goals. As a general rule, aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio per day, five days per week. You can gradually increase the length and intensity of your workouts as you get fitter.

Can I do cardio every day?

Yes, you can do cardio every day if you want to, as long as you're giving your body enough time to rest and recover. It's also important to vary the types of cardio you do to avoid overuse injuries and boredom.

Ultimately, the best time of day to do cardio is the time that works best for you and your schedule. Whether you prefer morning, afternoon, or evening workouts, the most important thing is to make exercise a consistent part of your routine. With time and dedication, you'll be well on your way to achieving your fitness goals.
