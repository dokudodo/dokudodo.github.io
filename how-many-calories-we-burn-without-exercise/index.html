How Many Calories Do We Burn Without Exercise?

The number of calories you burn without exercise varies depending on your age, sex, weight, and height. On average, a sedentary adult burns between 1,200 and 1,600 calories per day. This is known as your basal metabolic rate (BMR). Your BMR is the number of calories your body burns at rest to maintain basic bodily functions such as breathing, circulation, and temperature regulation.

It's important to note that your BMR is just an estimate, and it can vary based on a variety of factors. Additionally, this number does not take into account any physical activity you may do throughout the day, which can significantly increase your calorie burn.

Calculating Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

The most accurate way to calculate your BMR is to use a BMR calculator. These calculators take into account your age, sex, weight, and height to give you an estimate of how many calories you burn at rest. Here is a simple formula you can use to calculate your BMR:

BMR = 10 x weight (kg) + 6.25 x height (cm) - 5 x age (years) + 5 (for males) or -161 (for females)

For example, a 30-year-old female who weighs 68 kg and is 165 cm tall would have a BMR of approximately 1,423 calories per day.

Factors That Affect Calorie Burn Without Exercise

There are several factors that can affect the number of calories you burn without exercise:

  • Age: As you age, your metabolism slows down, which means you burn fewer calories at rest.
  • Sex: Men tend to have a higher BMR than women because they have more muscle mass and less body fat.
  • Weight: The more you weigh, the more calories you burn at rest because it takes more energy to maintain a larger body.
  • Height: Taller people tend to have a higher BMR because they have more surface area to maintain.
  • Body composition: Muscle burns more calories than fat, so people with more muscle mass tend to have a higher BMR.

Tips for Burning More Calories Throughout the Day

If you want to increase your calorie burn without exercise, there are several things you can do:

  • Stand more: Standing burns more calories than sitting, so try to stand up and move around as much as possible throughout the day.
  • Take the stairs: Taking the stairs instead of the elevator can significantly increase your calorie burn.
  • Drink more water: Drinking water can boost your metabolism and help you burn more calories.
  • Eat protein: Protein is more thermogenic than carbohydrates or fat, which means your body burns more calories digesting it.
  • Get enough sleep: Lack of sleep can slow down your metabolism and make it harder for you to burn calories.

FAQs

Q: How many calories do I burn while sleeping?

A: On average, you burn between 50 and 100 calories per hour while sleeping, depending on your weight and sex.

Q: Does drinking cold water burn more calories?

A: Drinking cold water can slightly increase your calorie burn because your body has to work harder to warm the water up to body temperature.

Q: Does stress burn calories?

A: Yes, stress can increase your calorie burn because it triggers the release of hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol, which can boost your metabolism.

Q: Can I lose weight without exercising?

A: Yes, you can lose weight without exercising by creating a calorie deficit through diet alone. However, exercise can help you burn more calories and improve your overall health.

Conclusion

Knowing how many calories you burn without exercise is an important step in understanding your metabolism and overall health. While your BMR is just an estimate, it can give you a good idea of how many calories you need to consume to maintain your weight. By making small changes to your daily routine, you can increase your calorie burn and improve your health.
