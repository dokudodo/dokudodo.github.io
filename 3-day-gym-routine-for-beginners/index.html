3 Day Gym Routine For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Alvine Jan 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Starting a gym routine can be intimidating, especially if you're new to working out. But with the right plan and mindset, you can achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health. In this article, we'll guide you through a 3 day gym routine for beginners. Whether you're looking to lose weight, gain muscle, or simply get in shape, this routine can help you get there.

Day 1: Upper Body

On the first day of your gym routine, focus on your upper body. Start with a warm-up of walking on the treadmill or using the elliptical for 10-15 minutes. Then, move on to these exercises:

  • Push-ups (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Bent-over Rows (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Overhead Press (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Bicep Curls (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Tricep Extensions (3 sets of 10 reps)

Remember to take breaks between each set and stretch your muscles afterwards. This will help prevent injuries and soreness.

Day 2: Lower Body

On the second day of your gym routine, focus on your lower body. Start with a warm-up of walking on the treadmill or using the elliptical for 10-15 minutes. Then, move on to these exercises:

  • Squats (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Lunges (3 sets of 10 reps on each leg)
  • Deadlifts (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Calf Raises (3 sets of 10 reps)

Again, take breaks between each set and stretch your muscles afterwards. You can also add some cardio exercises like cycling or using the stair climber to burn more calories.

Day 3: Full Body

On the third day of your gym routine, focus on your full body. This is a great way to challenge your muscles and boost your metabolism. Start with a warm-up of walking on the treadmill or using the elliptical for 10-15 minutes. Then, move on to these exercises:

  • Plank (3 sets of 30 seconds)
  • Mountain Climbers (3 sets of 20 reps)
  • Kettlebell Swings (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Lat Pulldowns (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Leg Press (3 sets of 10 reps)

After each set, take a break and stretch your muscles. You can also add some cardio exercises like jumping jacks or running on the treadmill.

Tips for Beginners

If you're new to working out, here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Start slow and gradually increase your intensity.
  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking water before, during, and after your workout.
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of protein, carbs, and healthy fats.
  • Listen to your body and don't push yourself too hard.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about a 3 day gym routine for beginners:

How often should I do this routine?

You can do this routine 3 days a week, with rest days in between. This will give your muscles time to recover and grow.

What if I can't do all the exercises?

It's okay to modify the exercises or use lighter weights if you're struggling. Focus on proper form and gradually increase your intensity over time.

How long should I rest between sets?

You should rest for 30-60 seconds between sets, depending on your fitness level and the intensity of the exercise.

Pros and Cons of a 3 Day Gym Routine

Here are some pros and cons of a 3 day gym routine for beginners:

Pros

  • It's a great way to get started with working out.
  • It focuses on different muscle groups to prevent boredom and overuse injuries.
  • It can help you build strength and endurance over time.

Cons

  • It may not be enough for advanced athletes or those with specific fitness goals.
  • It can be challenging for those who are new to working out.
  • It may not be effective if you don't follow a healthy diet and lifestyle outside of the gym.

Overall, a 3 day gym routine for beginners can be a great way to kickstart your fitness journey. With the right mindset and dedication, you can achieve your goals and improve your health.
