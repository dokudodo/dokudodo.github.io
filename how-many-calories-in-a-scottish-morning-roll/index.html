How Many Calories In A Scottish Morning Roll

Welcome to our blog about how many calories are in a Scottish morning roll. In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about this delicious breakfast treat, including its calorie content, how to make it, and some tips to make it healthier. So, let's get started!

Description

A Scottish morning roll is a type of bread roll that is a popular breakfast item in Scotland. It is a soft and fluffy white bread that is usually served warm and can be filled with various ingredients, such as bacon, eggs, sausage, or black pudding. It is a quick and easy breakfast option that is perfect for those who are always on the go.

How to Make a Scottish Morning Roll

Making a Scottish morning roll is easy, and you can do it at home. Here is a simple recipe that you can try:

Ingredients:

  • 500g strong white bread flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 sachet fast-action dried yeast
  • 300ml warm water

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast together.
  2. Add the warm water and mix until it forms a dough.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 10 minutes.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a damp cloth. Leave it to rise for an hour in a warm place.
  5. Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal pieces.
  6. Shape each piece into a ball and flatten it slightly.
  7. Place them on a baking tray and leave them to rise for another 30 minutes.
  8. Bake in a preheated oven at 200°C for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Let them cool slightly before serving.

How Many Calories in a Scottish Morning Roll?

A typical Scottish morning roll contains around 250-300 calories, depending on its size and the filling. If you add bacon, sausage, or black pudding, the calorie content will be higher. However, if you fill it with eggs and vegetables, it will be lower in calories.

Tips to Make a Healthier Scottish Morning Roll

If you want to make a healthier Scottish morning roll, you can try these tips:

  • Use wholemeal flour instead of white flour to increase the fiber content.
  • Fill it with eggs, vegetables, or lean meat instead of bacon or sausage.
  • Avoid adding butter or margarine to the roll.
  • Choose low-fat or fat-free dairy products to accompany the roll.

FAQ

Q: Can I freeze Scottish morning rolls?

A: Yes, you can freeze Scottish morning rolls. Wrap them in cling film or aluminium foil and place them in the freezer for up to three months. To reheat, thaw them overnight at room temperature and warm them up in the oven or microwave.

Q: What is the best filling for a Scottish morning roll?

A: The best filling for a Scottish morning roll is a matter of personal preference. Some popular options include bacon and eggs, sausage, black pudding, mushrooms, and tomatoes.

Pros and Cons of Scottish Morning Rolls

Pros:

  • Quick and easy breakfast option
  • Delicious and versatile
  • Satisfying and filling

Cons:

  • High in calories and fat
  • May contain processed meat
  • Not suitable for people with gluten intolerance or celiac disease
