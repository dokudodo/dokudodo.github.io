Are you wondering how many calories you burn naturally without exercising? The answer might surprise you! In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about your basal metabolic rate (BMR) and how many calories your body burns at rest.

What is BMR?

Your basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body burns at rest to maintain normal bodily functions such as breathing, circulating blood, and regulating body temperature. Even when you are not doing any physical activity, your body still requires energy to keep you alive.

How is BMR calculated?

BMR is calculated using several factors, including your age, gender, height, weight, and body composition. The most accurate way to determine your BMR is through a metabolic test, but you can also estimate it using various online BMR calculators.

Factors that affect BMR

Several factors can affect your BMR, including:

  • Age: As you age, your metabolism slows down, and your BMR decreases
  • Gender: Men generally have a higher BMR than women due to higher muscle mass
  • Height and weight: The taller and heavier you are, the higher your BMR
  • Body composition: Muscle tissue burns more calories at rest than fat tissue, so the more muscle you have, the higher your BMR
  • Hormones: Certain hormones, such as thyroid hormones, can affect your BMR

How many calories do you burn naturally?

The number of calories you burn naturally without exercise depends on your BMR. On average, a sedentary person with a BMR of 1500-2000 calories burns around 2000-2500 calories per day, including digesting food and other daily activities. However, this number can vary widely depending on your age, gender, height, weight, and body composition.

Tips to increase BMR

If you want to increase your BMR and burn more calories naturally, you can try the following tips:

  • Incorporate strength training into your workout routine to build more muscle mass
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of protein, fiber, and healthy fats
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Get enough sleep to allow your body to rest and recover
  • Reduce stress levels through relaxation techniques such as meditation or yoga

Pros and cons of BMR

There are several pros and cons to considering your BMR when it comes to weight loss and fitness:

Pros:

  • Knowing your BMR can help you determine how many calories you need to consume to maintain, gain, or lose weight
  • Increasing your BMR can help you burn more calories naturally and lose weight more efficiently

Cons:

  • Calculating your BMR can be time-consuming and require specialized equipment
  • Your BMR can fluctuate depending on various factors, making it difficult to always accurately predict how many calories you need

FAQ

What is the difference between BMR and metabolism?

Your metabolism refers to all the chemical processes in your body that convert food into energy. BMR is a specific part of your metabolism that relates to the number of calories your body burns at rest.

Can you increase your BMR?

Yes, you can increase your BMR through various lifestyle factors such as strength training, eating a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and reducing stress levels.

How do I calculate my BMR?

You can estimate your BMR using various online calculators or by getting a metabolic test done.

Now that you know how many calories you burn naturally without exercise, you can use this information to make informed decisions about your diet and exercise routine. Remember, every body is different, so it's essential to listen to your body and make adjustments as needed to achieve your health and fitness goals.
