How to Do Pelvic Floor Exercises After Birth

Pelvic floor exercises, also known as Kegels, involve contracting and relaxing the muscles that support your bladder, uterus, and rectum. These exercises can help strengthen these muscles, which can become weak after pregnancy and childbirth.

To do pelvic floor exercises, first find the muscles you need to work. You can do this by stopping the flow of urine midstream. Once you’ve found the muscles, you can do the exercises anywhere, anytime.

Step-by-Step Guide to Pelvic Floor Exercises After Birth

Follow these steps to do pelvic floor exercises after birth:

  1. Sit or lie down with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor.
  2. Tighten your pelvic floor muscles by squeezing as if you’re trying to stop the flow of urine or hold in gas.
  3. Hold the contraction for 3-5 seconds, then relax for the same amount of time.
  4. Repeat 10-15 times, or as many as you can do comfortably.
  5. Do the exercises at least 3 times a day.

Tips for Doing Pelvic Floor Exercises After Birth

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your pelvic floor exercises:

  • Start slow and gradually work your way up to longer hold times and more repetitions.
  • Don’t hold your breath while doing the exercises.
  • Try doing the exercises while lying down, sitting, and standing to work different muscle groups.
  • Make pelvic floor exercises a part of your daily routine, such as doing them while brushing your teeth or watching TV.

Solutions to Common Pelvic Floor Problems After Birth

Some women may experience pelvic floor problems after giving birth, such as incontinence or pain during sex. Here are some solutions to these common issues:

  • Incontinence: If you’re experiencing urine leakage, try doing pelvic floor exercises regularly. You can also talk to your doctor about other treatment options, such as medication or surgery.
  • Pain during sex: Pelvic floor exercises can help strengthen the muscles that support the vagina. You can also try different positions or using lubrication to make sex more comfortable.

FAQs About Pelvic Floor Exercises After Birth

Here are some frequently asked questions about pelvic floor exercises after birth:

How long should I do pelvic floor exercises after birth?

You should continue doing pelvic floor exercises for as long as you can to maintain strong muscles. Some experts recommend doing them for the rest of your life.

How soon after birth can I start doing pelvic floor exercises?

You can start doing pelvic floor exercises as soon as your doctor gives you the okay, usually around 4-6 weeks after giving birth.

Can I do pelvic floor exercises during pregnancy?

Yes, you can do pelvic floor exercises during pregnancy to help prepare your body for childbirth. Talk to your doctor or a physical therapist about the best exercises for you.

Pros and Cons of Pelvic Floor Exercises After Birth

Pros

  • Can help prevent or treat pelvic floor problems after birth
  • Easy to do anywhere, anytime
  • No equipment needed

Cons

  • May take time to see results
  • Can be difficult to find the right muscles at first
  • May not be effective for everyone

Overall, pelvic floor exercises are a safe and effective way to take care of your body after giving birth. By following the tips and solutions outlined in this article, you can maintain strong pelvic floor muscles and prevent common problems.
