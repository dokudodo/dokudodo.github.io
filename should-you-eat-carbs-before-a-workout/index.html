Should You Eat Carbs Before A Workout

Written by Bobby Feb 28, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to improve your exercise performance, one question that often comes up is whether or not you should eat carbs before a workout. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of eating carbs before exercising, and provide some tips on how to do it effectively.

Pros of Eating Carbs Before a Workout

Carbohydrates are the body's primary source of energy, and consuming them before a workout can help to improve your performance. By providing your muscles with a ready source of fuel, you'll be able to exercise for longer and with greater intensity.

In addition, eating carbs before a workout can help to prevent muscle breakdown. When you exercise, your body releases cortisol, a hormone that can break down muscle tissue. By consuming carbs before your workout, you can help to reduce the amount of cortisol that is released, which can help to preserve your muscle mass.

Finally, eating carbs before a workout can help to speed up your recovery time. When you exercise, your muscles use up their glycogen stores (the stored form of glucose). By consuming carbs before your workout, you can help to replenish these stores, which can reduce your recovery time and help you to feel less sore after your workout.

Cons of Eating Carbs Before a Workout

While there are many benefits to eating carbs before a workout, there are also some potential downsides to consider. For example, consuming too many carbs before a workout can lead to a drop in blood sugar levels, which can cause you to feel sluggish and fatigued during your workout.

In addition, consuming too many carbs before a workout can lead to digestive issues, such as bloating, cramping, and diarrhea. This is especially true if you're eating foods that are high in fiber, which can be difficult for your body to digest quickly.

How to Eat Carbs Before a Workout

If you decide that eating carbs before a workout is right for you, it's important to choose the right types of carbs. Complex carbs, such as whole grains, fruits, and vegetables, are a better choice than simple carbs, such as candy and soda.

You'll also want to time your carb intake properly. Eating a meal that is high in carbs two to three hours before your workout is ideal. This will give your body enough time to digest the food and convert it into energy.

If you don't have time to eat a full meal before your workout, a small snack that is high in carbs can also be effective. A banana, apple slices with peanut butter, or a granola bar are all good options.

Tips for Eating Carbs Before a Workout

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when eating carbs before a workout:

  • Choose complex carbs over simple carbs
  • Aim to eat your carb-rich meal two to three hours before your workout
  • If you don't have time for a full meal, eat a small snack that is high in carbs
  • Avoid foods that are high in fat, as they can slow down digestion
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated

FAQ

Q: Will eating carbs before a workout help me lose weight?

A: Eating carbs before a workout can help to improve your exercise performance, but it's not a magic solution for weight loss. To lose weight, you'll need to create a calorie deficit by consuming fewer calories than you burn.

Q: Can I eat carbs after a workout instead?

A: Absolutely! Consuming carbs after a workout can help to replenish your glycogen stores and speed up your recovery time. Just be sure to choose healthy sources of carbs, such as fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

Q: How many carbs should I eat before a workout?

A: The amount of carbs you should eat before a workout depends on your individual needs and goals. As a general rule, aim to consume 0.5 to 1 gram of carbs per pound of body weight.

Conclusion

Eating carbs before a workout can be beneficial for improving exercise performance, preventing muscle breakdown, and reducing recovery time. However, it's important to choose the right types of carbs and time your intake properly to avoid digestive issues and drops in blood sugar levels. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can ensure that you're fueling your body effectively before your next workout.
