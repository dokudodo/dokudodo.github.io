Things For Guys To Do In Vegas During The Day

When most people think of Las Vegas, they think of casinos, nightlife, and all things flashy. However, there are plenty of things for guys to do in Vegas during the day that don't involve gambling or partying. In this article, we'll explore some of the best options for men looking for a fun and exciting daytime experience in Sin City.

1. Hit the Shooting Range

If you're looking for a unique and exhilarating experience, consider visiting one of the many shooting ranges in Las Vegas. From handguns and shotguns to fully automatic weapons, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some of the most popular ranges include Machine Guns Vegas, The Range 702, and Battlefield Vegas. Just be sure to follow all safety protocols and listen carefully to the instructors.

2. Take a Helicopter Tour

If you're looking for a bird's-eye view of the city, consider taking a helicopter tour. There are several companies that offer tours of the Las Vegas Strip, Hoover Dam, and even the Grand Canyon. Some of the most popular tour operators include Maverick Helicopters and Papillon Grand Canyon Helicopters. Just be prepared for some turbulence and bring your camera to capture the stunning views.

3. Visit the Hoover Dam

The Hoover Dam is one of the most iconic landmarks in the United States and is located just 30 minutes from Las Vegas. Visitors can take a guided tour of the dam and learn about its history and engineering. There's also a visitor center with interactive exhibits and a movie theater. If you're feeling adventurous, consider taking a kayaking or rafting tour of the Colorado River below the dam.

4. Go Off-Roading in the Desert

If you're looking for an adrenaline rush, consider going off-roading in the desert. There are several companies that offer guided tours of the surrounding area, including the Mojave Desert and Valley of Fire State Park. You'll get to drive your own ATV or dune buggy and explore the rugged terrain. Just be sure to wear sunscreen and bring plenty of water.

5. Explore the Neon Museum

The Neon Museum is a unique and colorful museum that showcases the history of Las Vegas through its iconic neon signs. Visitors can take a guided tour of the outdoor exhibit and learn about the stories behind each sign. There are also several indoor exhibits that feature restored signs and other artifacts from the city's past.

6. Check out the High Roller Observation Wheel

The High Roller is the tallest observation wheel in the world and offers stunning views of the Las Vegas Strip. Each cabin can hold up to 40 people and includes a bar, making it a great option for groups. There are also several different ticket packages available, including a Happy Half Hour option that includes an open bar.

7. Visit the Mob Museum

The Mob Museum is a fascinating museum that explores the history of organized crime in America. Visitors can take a self-guided tour and learn about the rise and fall of some of the most notorious gangsters in history. There are also several interactive exhibits and artifacts on display, including weapons and clothing worn by mobsters.

8. Take a Trip to Red Rock Canyon

Red Rock Canyon is a stunning natural wonder located just 30 minutes from Las Vegas. Visitors can explore the scenic drive, go hiking, or even rock climbing. There's also a visitor center with exhibits and information about the area's wildlife and geology. If you're lucky, you might even spot a wild burro or bighorn sheep.

9. Try Indoor Skydiving

If you've ever wanted to experience the thrill of skydiving without jumping out of a plane, consider trying indoor skydiving. There are several companies that offer this unique experience in Las Vegas, including Vegas Indoor Skydiving and Flyaway Indoor Skydiving. You'll get to experience the sensation of freefalling in a safe and controlled environment.

10. Take a Tour of the Grand Canyon

If you have the time and budget, consider taking a tour of the Grand Canyon. There are several tour operators that offer day trips from Las Vegas, including helicopter tours, bus tours, and even a combination of both. You'll get to see one of the most breathtaking natural wonders in the world and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Conclusion

Las Vegas may be known for its nightlife, but there are plenty of things for guys to do in Vegas during the day. From shooting ranges and helicopter tours to museums and natural wonders, there's something for everyone. So the next time you're in Sin City, consider stepping outside of the casino and exploring all that the city has to offer.
