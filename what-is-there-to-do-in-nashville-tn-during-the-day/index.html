What Is There To Do In Nashville Tn During The Day

Welcome to Nashville, the capital of the state of Tennessee, where good music, good food, and good people come together. Nashville has been known as the “Music City” of the United States, where one can find the country’s most famous music venues, such as the Grand Ole Opry and the Ryman Auditorium. However, there’s more to Nashville than just music. In this article, we’ll take a look at what is there to do in Nashville TN during the day, from visiting museums to exploring parks.

Visit the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum

The Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum is a must-visit when in Nashville. It’s the world’s largest museum dedicated to country music and has exhibits that showcase the history and evolution of the genre. The museum also features a collection of over 2.5 million artifacts and memorabilia, including instruments, costumes, and recordings. Visitors can take a self-guided tour or join one of the guided tours.

Explore Centennial Park

Centennial Park is a 132-acre park located in the heart of Nashville. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy the outdoors, with its walking trails, gardens, and picnic areas. The park is also home to the Parthenon, a full-scale replica of the original Parthenon in Athens, Greece. Visitors can take a tour of the Parthenon and learn about its history and significance.

Visit the Cheekwood Estate and Gardens

The Cheekwood Estate and Gardens is a historic mansion and botanical garden located in Nashville. The mansion was built in 1929 and is now a museum that showcases American and European art from the 19th and 20th centuries. The botanical garden features over 55 acres of landscaped gardens, including a Japanese garden, a wildflower garden, and a sculpture trail.

Enjoy the Nashville Zoo

The Nashville Zoo is home to over 2,700 animals, including tigers, giraffes, and kangaroos. The zoo also has a variety of exhibits, such as the African savannah and the Gibbon Islands. Visitors can take a guided tour or explore the zoo on their own. The zoo also offers animal encounters and feeding opportunities.

Take a Walk on the Pedestrian Bridge

The John Seigenthaler Pedestrian Bridge is a landmark in Nashville and a great place to take a walk. The bridge spans the Cumberland River and offers stunning views of the city skyline. It’s also a popular spot for photography and picnics.

Visit the Adventure Science Center

The Adventure Science Center is a hands-on science museum that’s fun for all ages. The museum has over 175 exhibits that explore different areas of science, from space to energy. Visitors can also enjoy the planetarium and the IMAX theater.

Explore the Belle Meade Plantation

The Belle Meade Plantation is a historic mansion and museum that dates back to 1853. The mansion was once a thoroughbred horse farm and is now a museum that showcases the history of the property and the people who lived there. Visitors can take a tour of the mansion and grounds and learn about the plantation’s history and significance.

Take a Tour of the Ryman Auditorium

The Ryman Auditorium is a music venue that’s been around since 1892. It’s known as the “Mother Church of Country Music” and has hosted some of the biggest names in music, including Elvis Presley and Johnny Cash. Visitors can take a self-guided tour or join one of the guided tours and learn about the history of the venue.

Enjoy the Frist Art Museum

The Frist Art Museum is a contemporary art museum that features rotating exhibits from local and international artists. The museum is housed in a former post office and is known for its unique architecture. Visitors can take a self-guided tour or join one of the guided tours.

Shop at the Nashville Farmers’ Market

The Nashville Farmers’ Market is a great place to shop for fresh produce, meats, and cheeses. The market also has a variety of food vendors, including restaurants and cafes. Visitors can also enjoy live music and special events throughout the year.

Conclusion

There are plenty of things to do in Nashville TN during the day, from exploring museums to enjoying the outdoors. Whether you’re a music fan or not, there’s something for everyone in this vibrant city. So, plan your visit today and experience all that Nashville has to offer!
