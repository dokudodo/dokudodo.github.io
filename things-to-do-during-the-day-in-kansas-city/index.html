If you're planning a trip to Kansas City, you might be wondering what to do during the day. Don't worry, there are plenty of activities to keep you busy! In this article, we'll cover some of the best things to do in Kansas City during the day, including museums, parks, and more. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Museums
  • Parks

  • Food

Museums

Kansas City is home to several museums that are worth checking out during the day. The National World War I Museum and Memorial is a must-visit, with exhibits that cover the war from beginning to end. The Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art is another popular spot, with a collection that spans thousands of years and multiple cultures. The Arabia Steamboat Museum is also worth a visit, with artifacts recovered from a steamboat that sank in 1856.

Parks

If you're looking to enjoy some fresh air and outdoor activities, Kansas City has plenty of parks to choose from. Loose Park is a popular spot for picnics and walking trails, while Swope Park is home to the Kansas City Zoo and Starlight Theatre. The Kauffman Memorial Garden is a beautiful oasis in the middle of the city, with stunning floral displays and water features.

Shopping

For those who love to shop, Kansas City has several options for you. Country Club Plaza is a popular outdoor shopping district with a mix of high-end and affordable stores. The Legends Outlets is another great option, with over 100 stores and restaurants. If you're looking for something unique, head to the West Bottoms district, where you'll find antique shops and other quirky stores.

Food

Kansas City is known for its barbecue, so be sure to try some during your trip. Arthur Bryant's and Joe's Kansas City Bar-B-Que are two popular spots, but there are plenty of other options to choose from. If you're looking for something other than barbecue, check out some of the city's other culinary offerings, such as Vietnamese food in the River Market or Italian food in the Crossroads Arts District.

Sports

If you're a sports fan, Kansas City has plenty to offer. Catch a game at Kauffman Stadium, home of the Kansas City Royals, or at Arrowhead Stadium, home of the Kansas City Chiefs. If you're looking for something a little different, check out the National Collegiate Basketball Hall of Fame or the Negro Leagues Baseball Museum.

Conclusion

As you can see, there are plenty of things to do in Kansas City during the day. Whether you're interested in museums, parks, shopping, food, or sports, you'll find something to keep you entertained. Be sure to plan your itinerary ahead of time to make the most of your trip!
