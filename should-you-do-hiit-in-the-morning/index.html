Should You Do Hiit In The Morning

Written by Bowie Jul 22, 2023 · 4 min read

.

If you are looking for a way to boost your fitness routine, you may have heard of HIIT workouts. HIIT stands for High-Intensity Interval Training, and it involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. This type of workout is popular because it is efficient and effective. But should you do HIIT in the morning? In this article, we will explore the benefits and drawbacks of morning HIIT workouts.

  • Drawbacks of Doing HIIT in the Morning
  • How to Do HIIT in the Morning
  • Tips for Doing HIIT in the Morning

Benefits of Doing HIIT in the Morning

One of the biggest benefits of doing HIIT in the morning is that it can help jumpstart your metabolism for the day. When you exercise in the morning, your body continues to burn calories at a higher rate throughout the day. This can help you lose weight or maintain a healthy weight. Another benefit of doing HIIT in the morning is that it can help you feel more energized and focused throughout the day. Exercise releases endorphins, which are feel-good hormones that can improve your mood and mental clarity. Finally, doing HIIT in the morning can help you establish a consistent workout routine. When you exercise at the same time each day, it becomes a habit. This can help you stay motivated and committed to your fitness goals.

Drawbacks of Doing HIIT in the Morning

One potential drawback of doing HIIT in the morning is that it can be difficult to motivate yourself to exercise when you are still feeling groggy or sleepy. If you are not a morning person, you may find it challenging to get up and start moving. Another potential drawback of doing HIIT in the morning is that it can be hard on your body. When you first wake up, your muscles and joints may be stiff and sore. It is important to warm up properly before you begin your workout to avoid injury.

How to Do HIIT in the Morning

If you decide to do HIIT in the morning, it is important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. Begin with a warm-up that includes dynamic stretching and light cardio, such as jumping jacks or jogging in place. Next, choose a HIIT workout that includes exercises such as squats, lunges, push-ups, and burpees. Perform each exercise for 30 seconds, followed by 10 seconds of rest. Repeat the circuit for 3-4 rounds, and cool down with static stretching.

Tips for Doing HIIT in the Morning

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your morning HIIT workouts: - Get a good night's sleep to ensure you are well-rested and energized for your workout. - Eat a healthy breakfast that includes carbohydrates and protein to fuel your body. - Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts over time. - Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water before, during, and after your workout. - Use proper form when performing exercises to avoid injury.

FAQs

Q: Is it better to do HIIT in the morning or evening?
A: There is no right or wrong answer to this question. It depends on your personal preference and schedule. Some people prefer to exercise in the morning to jumpstart their metabolism, while others prefer to workout in the evening to relieve stress after a long day. Q: How long should a HIIT workout be?
A: A typical HIIT workout should last between 20-30 minutes, including warm-up and cool-down periods.

Pros and Cons of Doing HIIT in the Morning

Pros: - Jumpstarts metabolism for the day - Helps you feel more energized and focused throughout the day - Establishes a consistent workout routine Cons: - Can be difficult to motivate yourself in the morning - Can be hard on your body if you don't warm up properly In conclusion, there are both benefits and drawbacks to doing HIIT in the morning. If you are a morning person and enjoy starting your day with a workout, it can be a great way to jumpstart your metabolism and establish a consistent fitness routine. However, if you struggle to get motivated in the morning or have trouble with stiff muscles and joints, it may be better to opt for an evening workout instead. Ultimately, the most important thing is to find a workout routine that works for you and your lifestyle.
