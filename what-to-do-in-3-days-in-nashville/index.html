If you're planning a trip to Nashville and have only three days to explore, don't worry! Nashville has plenty of attractions and activities to offer visitors, even if you're on a tight schedule. In this guide, we'll show you how to make the most of your three days in Nashville, including what to do, where to eat, and where to stay. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Day 1: Downtown Nashville
  • Day 2: Music and History
  • Day 3: Nature and Shopping

Day 1: Downtown Nashville

Start your first day in Nashville by exploring the downtown area. This part of the city is known for its lively music scene, historic landmarks, and famous honky-tonks. Here are some of the top things to do:

Visit the Country Music Hall of Fame

The Country Music Hall of Fame is a must-see attraction for any music lover. This museum features exhibits on the history of country music, including displays on famous musicians like Johnny Cash, Dolly Parton, and Hank Williams. You can easily spend a few hours exploring the museum and listening to recordings of classic country songs.

Take a Walk Along Broadway

Broadway is the main street in downtown Nashville and is home to numerous honky-tonks, bars, and live music venues. Take a stroll down this street and listen to the country music coming from the bars. You might even find a new favorite musician!

Visit the Ryman Auditorium

The Ryman Auditorium is one of the most iconic music venues in the United States. This historic building has hosted some of the biggest names in music, including Elvis Presley, Johnny Cash, and Patsy Cline. Take a tour of the auditorium and learn about its rich history.

Day 2: Music and History

On your second day in Nashville, focus on exploring the city's music and history. Here are some of the top things to do:

Visit the Johnny Cash Museum

The Johnny Cash Museum is a must-visit attraction for any fan of "The Man in Black." This museum features exhibits on Cash's life and career, including artifacts like his guitars and stage costumes. You can even see the famous black suit he wore in the "Hurt" music video.

Take a Tour of the Belle Meade Plantation

The Belle Meade Plantation is a historic mansion that was once a thriving thoroughbred horse farm. Take a tour of the mansion and learn about the history of the property. You can also sample some of the wines produced at the on-site winery.

Visit the Frist Art Museum

The Frist Art Museum is located in a historic post office building and features rotating exhibits of contemporary and classic art. Check their website to see what exhibits are currently on display.

Day 3: Nature and Shopping

On your final day in Nashville, take a break from the music and history and explore the city's natural beauty and shopping scene. Here are some ideas:

Visit the Cheekwood Estate and Gardens

The Cheekwood Estate and Gardens is a stunning 55-acre property that includes a historic mansion and beautifully landscaped gardens. Take a stroll through the gardens and enjoy the beauty of the property.

Shop at the Opry Mills Mall

The Opry Mills Mall is one of the largest shopping centers in Nashville and includes a wide variety of stores, restaurants, and entertainment options. Spend a few hours browsing the shops and finding some souvenirs to take home.

Take a Hike at Radnor Lake State Park

Radnor Lake State Park is located just outside of Nashville and offers scenic hiking trails and a beautiful lake. Take a hike and enjoy the natural beauty of Tennessee.

Where to Eat in Nashville

Nashville is known for its delicious southern cuisine, so make sure to try some of these local favorites:

  • Hot chicken at Hattie B's
  • Barbecue at Martin's BBQ
  • Biscuits at Loveless Cafe
  • Meat and three sides at Arnold's Country Kitchen

Where to Stay in Nashville

If you're looking for a place to stay in Nashville, consider one of these options:

  • The Hermitage Hotel
  • The Omni Nashville Hotel
  • The Thompson Nashville
  • The Gaylord Opryland Resort and Convention Center

Conclusion

With so many attractions and activities, Nashville is a great destination for a three-day trip. Whether you're interested in music history, shopping, or outdoor activities, there's something for everyone in this vibrant city. Plan your itinerary carefully, and you'll be sure to make the most of your time in Nashville!
