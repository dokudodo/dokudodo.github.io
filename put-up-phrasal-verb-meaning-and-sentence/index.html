Definition of "Put Up"

"Put up" is a phrasal verb that means to erect or place something in a particular position or place. It can also mean to tolerate or endure something, or to accommodate someone by providing them with a place to stay.

Usage of "Put Up"

"Put up" is a versatile phrasal verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. Here are some common ways to use "put up" in a sentence:

  • To erect or place something: "I'm going to put up some shelves in my room."
  • To tolerate or endure something: "I can't put up with this noise anymore."
  • To accommodate someone by providing them with a place to stay: "I'm going to put my guests up in the spare room."

Examples of "Put Up"

Here are some examples of "put up" used in sentences:

  • "I need to put up some curtains in my bedroom."
  • "I can't put up with this traffic anymore."
  • "I'm going to put up my sister for the weekend."

Tips for using "Put Up" correctly

Here are some tips to help you use "put up" correctly:

  • Make sure you use the correct preposition after "put up". For example, you should say "I'm going to put up with" rather than "I'm going to put up".
  • Be careful not to confuse "put up" with "put on", which has a different meaning.
  • If you're unsure about how to use "put up" correctly, try to find examples of it being used in context and learn from them.

FAQs about "Put Up"

What's the difference between "put up" and "put on"?

"Put up" means to erect or place something, while "put on" means to wear or apply something.

Can "put up" be used to mean "postpone"?

No, "put up" is not commonly used to mean "postpone".

Pros and cons of using "Put Up"

Pros

  • "Put up" is a versatile phrasal verb that can be used in a variety of contexts.
  • It's a common phrasal verb that you're likely to encounter in everyday conversations.

Cons

  • It can be difficult to remember the correct preposition to use after "put up".
  • Using "put up" incorrectly can lead to confusion or misunderstandings.

In conclusion, "put up" is a useful phrasal verb to know if you want to improve your English language skills. By understanding its meaning and usage, you can use it correctly in a variety of contexts and sound more fluent and natural in your conversations.
