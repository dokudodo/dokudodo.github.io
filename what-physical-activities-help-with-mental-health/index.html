What Physical Activities Help With Mental Health A Guide To Improving Your Well Being

Physical activity has been proven to have numerous benefits for both the body and mind. Engaging in regular exercise can not only help you maintain a healthy weight and reduce your risk of chronic diseases, but it can also improve your mental health. In this article, we’ll discuss what physical activities help with mental health and how you can incorporate them into your daily routine.

Cardiovascular Exercise

Cardiovascular exercise, also known as cardio, is any form of activity that elevates your heart rate and gets your blood pumping. This type of exercise is great for improving your mental health because it releases endorphins, which are natural mood-boosters. Some examples of cardio exercises include:

  • Running or jogging
  • Cycling
  • Swimming
  • Rowing
  • Dancing

Try to aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio exercise per day, five days a week. You can break this up into smaller increments throughout the day if needed, such as three 10-minute sessions.

Strength Training

Strength training involves using weights, resistance bands, or your own body weight to build muscle and improve your overall strength. This type of exercise has been shown to have a positive impact on mental health by reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression. Some examples of strength training exercises include:

  • Squats
  • Lunges
  • Push-ups
  • Planks
  • Deadlifts

Try to incorporate strength training exercises into your routine two to three times per week.

Yoga

Yoga is a mind-body practice that combines physical postures, breathing techniques, and meditation. This type of exercise has been shown to improve mental health by reducing stress, anxiety, and depression. Some benefits of practicing yoga include:

  • Improved flexibility and balance
  • Reduced inflammation
  • Lowered blood pressure
  • Increased relaxation

Try to practice yoga at least once a week, whether it's in a class or at home with a video or app.

Pilates

Pilates is a low-impact form of exercise that focuses on strengthening the core muscles, improving posture, and increasing flexibility. This type of exercise has been shown to have a positive impact on mental health by reducing stress and anxiety. Some benefits of practicing Pilates include:

  • Improved posture and alignment
  • Reduced risk of injury
  • Increased strength and flexibility
  • Reduced stress and anxiety

Try to incorporate Pilates into your routine two to three times per week.

Outdoor Activities

Spending time outdoors has been shown to have numerous benefits for mental health, including reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression. Some outdoor activities you can try include:

  • Hiking
  • Walking or jogging
  • Biking
  • Swimming
  • Kayaking or canoeing

Try to spend at least 30 minutes outdoors each day, whether it's going for a walk or doing an outdoor activity.

Dancing

Dancing is a fun and enjoyable form of exercise that can have a positive impact on mental health. Not only does it release endorphins, but it can also improve self-esteem and body image. Some types of dancing you can try include:

  • Zumba
  • Ballet
  • Hip-hop
  • Salsa
  • Swing

Try to incorporate dancing into your routine at least once a week, whether it's taking a class or dancing at home.

Sports

Participating in team sports can not only provide physical benefits, but it can also have a positive impact on mental health. Being part of a team can improve social connections and reduce feelings of loneliness and isolation. Some team sports you can try include:

  • Basketball
  • Soccer
  • Volleyball
  • Softball
  • Ultimate Frisbee

Try to participate in a team sport at least once a week.

Tips for Incorporating Physical Activity into Your Routine

If you're new to physical activity or have a busy schedule, it can be challenging to find time to exercise. Here are some tips for incorporating physical activity into your routine:

  • Start small: Begin with short, 10-minute sessions and gradually increase your time and intensity.
  • Find an activity you enjoy: If you don't enjoy the exercise you're doing, you're less likely to stick with it.
  • Make it a social activity: Exercise with a friend or join a class to make it more enjoyable.
  • Set goals: Whether it's running a 5K or lifting a certain weight, setting goals can help keep you motivated and on track.
  • Stay consistent: Try to exercise at the same time each day to make it a habit.

FAQs

What is the best type of exercise for mental health?

Any type of physical activity can have a positive impact on mental health, but some of the best types include cardio, strength training, yoga, Pilates, and outdoor activities.

How much exercise do I need to improve my mental health?

Try to aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise per day, five days a week. You can break this up into smaller increments throughout the day if needed.

Can exercise help with anxiety and depression?

Yes, exercise has been shown to reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression by releasing endorphins and reducing stress levels.

What are some tips for staying motivated to exercise?

Some tips for staying motivated include setting goals, finding an activity you enjoy, exercising with a friend or joining a class, and staying consistent with your routine.

Incorporating physical activity into your daily routine can have numerous benefits for both your physical and mental health. Whether it's cardio, strength training, yoga, or outdoor activities, find an activity you enjoy and make it a habit. By taking care of your body, you're also taking care of your mind.
