Does Exercise Make You Gain Weight At First

Written by Jordan May 17, 2023 · 4 min read

In today’s world, people are more concerned about their fitness and health than ever before. Exercise is one of the most popular ways to maintain a healthy lifestyle. However, there is a common myth that exercise can make you gain weight at first. In this article, we will explore whether this is true or false and provide tips on how to avoid gaining weight while exercising.

Table of Contents

  • Introduction
  • How to Start Exercising

Introduction

If you are new to exercise, it can be intimidating to start. You may have heard that exercise can cause you to gain weight at first. This can be discouraging and may even prevent you from starting your fitness journey. However, the truth is that exercise does not cause you to gain weight at first. The weight gain is usually due to other factors such as muscle gain or water retention.

How to Start Exercising

If you are new to exercise, it is important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity. This will help you avoid injury and prevent burnout. Some good exercises for beginners include walking, jogging, swimming, and cycling. It is also important to incorporate strength training into your routine to build muscle and improve your overall fitness.

Step 1: Set Realistic Goals

Before starting any exercise program, it is important to set realistic goals. This will help you stay motivated and on track. Your goals should be specific, measurable, and achievable. For example, instead of saying “I want to lose weight,” set a goal to lose 10 pounds in the next three months.

Step 2: Choose the Right Exercise

Choosing the right exercise is important to ensure that you enjoy your workout and stick with it. It is also important to choose an exercise that fits your fitness level and goals. For example, if you want to build muscle, incorporate strength training into your routine. If you want to improve your cardiovascular health, focus on aerobic exercises such as running or cycling.

Step 3: Create a Workout Plan

Creating a workout plan is essential to ensure that you are getting the most out of your exercise routine. Your plan should include a combination of cardio and strength training exercises. It is also important to vary your routine to prevent boredom and to challenge your body.

Does Exercise Make You Gain Weight at First?

The short answer is no, exercise does not cause you to gain weight at first. In fact, exercise can help you lose weight and improve your overall health. However, it is not uncommon to gain weight when you first start exercising. This weight gain is usually due to other factors such as muscle gain and water retention.

Pros of Weight Gain from Exercise

Weight gain from exercise can be a good thing, especially if it is due to muscle gain. Muscle is more dense than fat, so even if you gain weight, you may be losing inches. Muscle also burns more calories than fat, so the more muscle you have, the more calories you burn at rest.

Cons of Weight Gain from Exercise

Weight gain from exercise can also be a bad thing if it is due to unhealthy habits such as overeating or not getting enough rest. It is important to maintain a healthy diet and get enough rest to avoid gaining weight while exercising.

Tips to Avoid Gaining Weight While Exercising

If you are concerned about gaining weight while exercising, there are several tips you can follow to avoid this:

Tip 1: Monitor Your Caloric Intake

It is important to monitor your caloric intake to avoid overeating. This can be done by tracking your meals and snacks in a food journal or using a calorie tracking app.

Tip 2: Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that includes fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and whole grains can help you maintain a healthy weight while exercising.

Tip 3: Drink Plenty of Water

Drinking plenty of water can help you stay hydrated and prevent water retention, which can cause weight gain.

Tip 4: Get Enough Rest

Getting enough rest is important to allow your body to recover from exercise and prevent fatigue. Lack of rest can also lead to overeating and weight gain.

FAQs

Q1: Can exercise cause weight gain?

A: No, exercise does not cause weight gain. However, it is not uncommon to gain weight when you first start exercising due to factors such as muscle gain and water retention.

Q2: How much exercise should I do per week?

A: It is recommended that adults get at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week.

Conclusion

Exercise does not cause you to gain weight at first. Weight gain is usually due to other factors such as muscle gain and water retention. By following a balanced diet, monitoring your caloric intake, and getting enough rest, you can avoid gaining weight while exercising. Remember to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity to avoid injury and prevent burnout. With dedication and commitment, you can achieve your fitness goals and maintain a healthy lifestyle.
