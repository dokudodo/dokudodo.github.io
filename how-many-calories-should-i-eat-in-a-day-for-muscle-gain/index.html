How To Determine Your Caloric Needs

Before we dive into how many calories you should eat in a day for muscle gain, we need to determine your caloric needs. Your caloric needs will vary depending on your age, sex, weight, height, and activity level. There are a few ways to calculate your caloric needs, including the Harris-Benedict equation, the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, and the Katch-McArdle equation.

Harris-Benedict Equation

The Harris-Benedict equation is one of the most commonly used equations to determine caloric needs. It takes into account your weight, height, age, and sex. Here is the equation:

BMR = 88.362 + (13.397 x weight in kg) + (4.799 x height in cm) – (5.677 x age in years)

Once you have your BMR, you can multiply it by your activity level to determine your total daily energy expenditure (TDEE).

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is a more recent equation that takes into account your weight, height, age, and sex. Here is the equation:

BMR = (10 x weight in kg) + (6.25 x height in cm) – (5 x age in years) + 5

Once you have your BMR, you can multiply it by your activity level to determine your TDEE.

Katch-McArdle Equation

The Katch-McArdle equation takes into account your body fat percentage. Here is the equation:

BMR = 370 + (21.6 x lean mass in kg)

Once you have your BMR, you can multiply it by your activity level to determine your TDEE.

Step-by-Step Guide to Calculating Your Caloric Needs

Now that you know the equations for calculating your BMR, let's walk through a step-by-step guide to calculating your caloric needs.

  1. Calculate your BMR using one of the equations above.
  2. Determine your activity level. Are you sedentary, lightly active, moderately active, very active, or extra active?
  3. Multiply your BMR by your activity level to determine your TDEE.
  4. If you want to gain muscle, add 250-500 calories to your TDEE. This will create a calorie surplus that will allow your body to build muscle.

Tips for Building Muscle Mass

In addition to eating the right number of calories, there are some tips you should follow to build muscle mass. Here are a few:

  • Strength train regularly
  • Eat enough protein
  • Get enough sleep
  • Stay hydrated
  • Be consistent

The Solution to Building Muscle Mass

The solution to building muscle mass is simple: eat enough calories, strength train regularly, and follow the tips we mentioned above. It takes time and consistency to build muscle, but if you stick with it, you will see results.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories should I eat to build muscle?

To build muscle, you should eat 250-500 calories above your TDEE.

What is the best way to build muscle?

The best way to build muscle is to eat enough calories, strength train regularly, and follow the tips we mentioned above.

How long does it take to build muscle?

It takes time and consistency to build muscle. You may start to see results in a few weeks, but it can take several months or even years to build significant muscle mass.

Cons
