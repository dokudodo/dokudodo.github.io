Hi Tea Menu Items A Guide To Delicious Snacks

If you're looking for a light meal or a mid-day snack, hi tea is the perfect option. Hi tea, also known as afternoon tea, originated in the UK and has become a popular tradition around the world. It typically includes a variety of tea, sandwiches, scones, and desserts. In this article, we'll explore some of the most popular hi tea menu items and how to make them.

What is Hi Tea?

Hi tea is a light meal typically served between lunch and dinner, around 4 pm. It usually consists of tea, sandwiches, scones, and cakes. The tradition dates back to the 19th century when the British aristocracy would have tea and snacks to tide them over until dinner. Today, hi tea is enjoyed by people of all ages and is often served in hotels, cafes, and restaurants.

Hi Tea Menu Items

Tea

The star of the show is, of course, the tea. It's usually served in a teapot with a side of milk, sugar, and lemon. Some popular tea varieties include Earl Grey, English Breakfast, Darjeeling, and Assam. Herbal teas like chamomile and mint are also great options.

Sandwiches

Sandwiches are a staple of hi tea and come in a variety of flavors. Some popular options include cucumber and cream cheese, egg and mayo, smoked salmon and cream cheese, and ham and mustard. The bread used is typically white or brown, and the crusts are cut off for a more refined look.

Scones

Scones are a must-have at hi tea. They're a type of bread that's slightly sweet and served with jam and clotted cream. Some variations include fruit scones and cheese scones. They're usually served warm and freshly baked.

Cakes and Pastries

No hi tea is complete without a selection of cakes and pastries. Some popular options include macarons, cupcakes, mini tarts, and eclairs. They're usually bite-sized and perfect for sharing.

How to Make Hi Tea Menu Items

Sandwiches

To make cucumber and cream cheese sandwiches, you'll need: - Sliced white bread - Cream cheese - Cucumber - Salt and pepper Instructions: 1. Spread cream cheese on one slice of bread. 2. Slice the cucumber thinly and place it on top of the cream cheese. 3. Sprinkle with salt and pepper. 4. Top with another slice of bread. 5. Cut into bite-sized pieces and serve.

Scones

To make scones, you'll need: - 225g self-raising flour - 50g butter - 25g caster sugar - 150ml milk - A pinch of salt Instructions: 1. Preheat the oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7. 2. Rub the butter into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs. 3. Stir in the sugar and salt. 4. Add the milk and stir until a dough forms. 5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut into rounds. 6. Place on a baking tray and brush with milk. 7. Bake for 12-15 minutes until golden. 8. Serve warm with jam and clotted cream.

Cakes and Pastries

To make mini tarts, you'll need: - Ready-made pastry - Jam - Fresh fruit - Whipped cream Instructions: 1. Preheat the oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6. 2. Roll out the pastry and cut into circles. 3. Press into a muffin tin. 4. Add a spoonful of jam to each tart. 5. Top with fresh fruit. 6. Bake for 15-20 minutes until golden. 7. Allow to cool, then top with whipped cream.

Tips for Hosting Hi Tea

- Choose a variety of teas to cater to different tastes. - Offer a selection of savory and sweet items. - Keep portions small so guests can try everything. - Use a tiered cake stand for a more elegant look. - Decorate the table with flowers and vintage crockery for a traditional feel.

Pros and Cons of Hi Tea

Pros: - A great way to socialize with friends and family. - A lighter, more affordable alternative to a full meal. - A chance to try new teas and snacks. Cons: - Can be expensive in some venues. - Not suitable for those with dietary restrictions. - Can be time-consuming to prepare at home.

FAQs

What is the difference between hi tea and afternoon tea?

Hi tea and afternoon tea are often used interchangeably, but there is a subtle difference. Hi tea is a more substantial meal that includes savory items like sandwiches and pastries, while afternoon tea is more focused on sweets like cakes and scones.

What should I wear to hi tea?

Most hi tea venues have a smart-casual dress code, so dress up a little but don't feel like you need to wear a ballgown. Avoid wearing jeans or sneakers.

Is it okay to ask for a refill of tea?

Yes, it's perfectly fine to ask for a refill of tea. In fact, it's expected in most venues. Just ask your server politely and they'll be happy to oblige.

Hi tea is a great way to enjoy a light meal and catch up with friends. Whether you're hosting at home or visiting a venue, there are plenty of delicious snacks to choose from. Don't forget to try a variety of teas and experiment with different flavors!
