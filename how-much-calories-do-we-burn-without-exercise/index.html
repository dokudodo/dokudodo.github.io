How Many Calories Do We Burn Without Exercise?

On average, a sedentary person burns around 1,600 to 2,000 calories per day. This calorie burn is known as your basal metabolic rate (BMR), which is the amount of energy your body needs to perform basic functions such as breathing, circulating blood, and regulating temperature. Your BMR is affected by several factors, including age, gender, weight, and height.

Here's a breakdown of how many calories you burn without exercise based on your age and gender:

Tracking Your Calorie Burn

If you're trying to lose weight, it's important to track your calorie intake and calorie burn. There are several ways to track your calorie burn, including:

  • Using a fitness tracker or smartwatch that tracks your daily activity and calorie burn
  • Using a calorie tracking app that allows you to input your daily activity and estimate your calorie burn
  • Using a heart rate monitor during exercise to get a more accurate estimate of your calorie burn

Remember that these tools are just estimates, and your actual calorie burn may be different depending on your individual factors.

Tips to Increase Your Calorie Burn

If you want to increase your calorie burn, there are several things you can do:

  • Move more throughout the day by taking frequent breaks to stand up and stretch
  • Incorporate more physical activity into your daily routine, such as taking the stairs instead of the elevator or going for a walk during your lunch break
  • Add strength training to your exercise routine, as building muscle can increase your BMR
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, as protein can also increase your BMR

Remember that increasing your calorie burn alone won't necessarily lead to weight loss; you also need to be in a calorie deficit by consuming fewer calories than you burn.

FAQ

How much calories do we burn while sleeping?

On average, a person burns around 50-100 calories per hour while sleeping. So, if you sleep for 8 hours, you could burn around 400-800 calories.

Does our calorie burn decrease as we age?

Yes, our calorie burn typically decreases as we age due to a decrease in muscle mass and a slower metabolism. This is why it's important to stay active and maintain a healthy diet as we get older.

Can we increase our calorie burn by drinking more water?

Drinking water can help with weight loss by increasing feelings of fullness and reducing calorie intake, but it doesn't directly increase calorie burn.

In conclusion, we burn calories all the time, even when we're not exercising. By understanding how many calories we burn without exercise and taking steps to increase our calorie burn, we can achieve our weight loss goals and live a healthier life.
