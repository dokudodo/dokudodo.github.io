What Time Is Early Morning Prayer

Introduction

As a Muslim, one of the five pillars of Islam is to perform the five daily prayers. The early morning prayer, also known as Fajr, is one of these prayers. Performing Fajr on time is very important as it is a way to start your day with the remembrance of Allah. In this article, we will discuss what time is early morning prayer, how to perform it, and the benefits of doing so.

How to Perform Early Morning Prayer

Before we discuss the timing of early morning prayer, let's first understand how to perform it. Here is a step-by-step guide:

1. Make Wudu

Wudu is the Islamic way of washing your face, arms, and feet before prayer. It is essential to perform wudu before every prayer. You can learn more about how to perform wudu here.

2. Face the Qiblah

The Qiblah is the direction towards the Kaaba in Mecca, Saudi Arabia. Muslims face the Qiblah during prayer. You can use a compass or a Qiblah app to find the direction of the Qiblah.

3. Start the Prayer

Stand up straight and say the opening takbir: “Allahu Akbar” (Allah is the Greatest).

Recite Surah Al-Fatiha, the first chapter of the Quran.

Recite another portion of the Quran or a prayer.

Bow down and say “Subhana rabbiyal azeem” (Glory be to my Lord, the Almighty).

Stand up straight and say “Sami allahu liman hamidah” (Allah hears those who praise Him).

Prostrate and say “Subhana rabbiyal a'la” (Glory be to my Lord, the Most High).

Sit up straight and say “Allahu Akbar” (Allah is the Greatest).

Prostrate again and say “Subhana rabbiyal a'la” (Glory be to my Lord, the Most High).

Stand up straight and say “Allahu Akbar” (Allah is the Greatest).

Repeat steps 4-9 for the second rak'ah (cycle) of the prayer.

After you finish the second rak'ah, sit and recite the Tashahhud, a prayer that is recited in every prayer.

End the prayer by saying the Taslim, “As-Salamu Alaykum Wa Rahmatullah” (Peace be upon you and the mercy of Allah).

The Timing of Early Morning Prayer

Now that we know how to perform early morning prayer, let's discuss the timing of Fajr. Early morning prayer can be performed between the time of dawn and sunrise. Dawn is defined as the time when the first light appears on the horizon, before the sun rises. The time of dawn varies depending on your location and the season. You can use a prayer time calculator or an Islamic calendar to find the exact timing of Fajr in your area.

Benefits of Early Morning Prayer

Performing early morning prayer has numerous benefits, both spiritual and physical. Here are some of the benefits:

  • Starting your day with the remembrance of Allah
  • Feeling a sense of peace and tranquility
  • Strengthening your faith
  • Improving your focus and productivity throughout the day
  • Boosting your immune system and metabolism
  • Improving your sleep quality

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about early morning prayer:

What happens if I miss the early morning prayer?

If you miss the early morning prayer, you can make it up later in the day. However, it is best to perform the prayer on time.

Do I have to wake up before dawn to perform early morning prayer?

Yes, it is recommended to wake up before dawn to perform early morning prayer. It may be difficult at first, but with practice, it will become easier.

What if I am traveling and cannot find the direction of the Qiblah?

If you cannot find the direction of the Qiblah, you can estimate the direction to the best of your ability. Allah is merciful and understanding.

In conclusion, early morning prayer is an important part of a Muslim's daily routine. It is important to perform the prayer on time and to the best of your ability. By doing so, you will reap the spiritual and physical benefits of this beautiful act of worship.
