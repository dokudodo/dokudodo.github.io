When you have surgery scheduled, it's essential to take care of yourself in the days leading up to the procedure. One question that may come up is whether it's safe to exercise the day before surgery. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of working out before surgery, offer tips to help you decide, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Pros and Cons of Working Out Before Surgery

There are both pros and cons to working out before surgery. On the one hand, exercise can help you reduce stress, improve circulation, and keep your body in good condition. On the other hand, working out too close to surgery can increase your risk of complications like bleeding, infection, and delayed healing.

Ultimately, the decision to exercise before surgery depends on your individual health status, the type of surgery you're having, and your surgeon's advice. Before making a decision, it's important to talk to your doctor and follow their recommendations.

How to Decide if You Should Exercise Before Surgery

If you're wondering whether it's safe to work out before surgery, here are some factors to consider:

  • The type of surgery you're having
  • Your overall health and fitness level
  • Your surgeon's recommendations
  • The timing of your surgery

Based on these factors, your doctor can help you decide whether exercise is safe for you before surgery. They may recommend that you avoid exercise for a certain period before the procedure or suggest specific types of workouts that are safe for you to do.

Step-by-Step Guide to Pre-Surgery Workouts

If your doctor has given you the green light to exercise before surgery, here are some steps you can take to stay safe:

  1. Start slow: Don't try to do too much too soon. Ease into your workouts gradually to avoid injury.
  2. Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workouts to stay hydrated.
  3. Avoid high-impact activities: Activities like running and jumping can increase your risk of injury. Stick to low-impact exercises like walking, cycling, or swimming.
  4. Listen to your body: If you feel pain or discomfort during your workouts, stop immediately and talk to your doctor.

Tips for Staying Safe Before Surgery

Here are some additional tips to help you stay safe before your surgery:

  • Follow your doctor's instructions: Be sure to follow any pre-surgery instructions your doctor gives you, such as avoiding certain foods or medications.
  • Get plenty of rest: Make sure you're getting enough sleep in the days leading up to your surgery.
  • Avoid alcohol and tobacco: These substances can increase your risk of complications during surgery.
  • Stay positive: Try to stay calm and positive before your surgery. High stress levels can increase your risk of complications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How soon before surgery should I stop exercising?

A: The timing of when to stop exercising before surgery depends on the type of surgery you're having and your individual health status. Your doctor can give you specific guidelines.

Q: Can exercise help me recover from surgery faster?

A: In some cases, exercise can be beneficial for recovery after surgery. However, it's important to talk to your doctor before starting any post-surgery exercise program.

Q: Can I do yoga before surgery?

A: Yoga can be a good pre-surgery workout option, as long as you avoid poses that put pressure on your abdomen. Talk to your doctor and your yoga instructor before starting a pre-surgery yoga practice.

The Bottom Line

Working out before surgery can be a good way to reduce stress and keep your body in good condition. However, it's important to talk to your doctor and follow their recommendations to ensure that you stay safe and avoid complications. By taking the right steps, you can help ensure a successful surgery and a smooth recovery.
