Are you curious about how many calories you burn in a day without exercise? The answer depends on a variety of factors, such as your age, gender, height, weight, and lifestyle habits. In this article, we will discuss the average number of calories burned each day without exercise and some tips to increase your calorie burn.

  • Factors Affecting Calorie Burn

Average Calorie Burn

The average number of calories burned each day without exercise is around 1,600 to 2,400 for women and 2,000 to 3,000 for men. This number is known as your basal metabolic rate (BMR), which is the amount of energy your body needs to maintain basic functions such as breathing, circulation, and digestion. Your BMR accounts for around 60 to 75 percent of your total daily calorie burn.

In addition to your BMR, you also burn calories through physical activity, such as walking, cleaning, and gardening. This is known as your non-exercise activity thermogenesis (NEAT) and can account for up to 15 percent of your total daily calorie burn.

Factors Affecting Calorie Burn

Several factors can affect your daily calorie burn, including:

  • Age: As you age, your BMR decreases.
  • Gender: Men typically have a higher BMR than women due to their higher muscle mass.
  • Height: Taller individuals have a higher BMR than shorter individuals.
  • Weight: Heavier individuals have a higher BMR than lighter individuals.
  • Body composition: Muscle burns more calories than fat, so individuals with more muscle mass have a higher BMR.
  • Hormones: Thyroid hormones and sex hormones can affect your BMR.
  • Genetics: Some individuals have a naturally higher or lower BMR.

Tips to Increase Calorie Burn

While your BMR accounts for the majority of your daily calorie burn, there are several ways to increase your calorie burn without exercise:

  • Eat protein: Your body burns more calories digesting protein than it does digesting carbohydrates or fat.
  • Drink water: Drinking water can increase your calorie burn by around 24 to 30 percent for an hour after drinking.
  • Get enough sleep: Lack of sleep can decrease your BMR and increase your hunger hormones, leading to overeating.
  • Stand more: Sitting for long periods can decrease your NEAT, so try to stand or move around every hour.
  • Take the stairs: Taking the stairs instead of the elevator can increase your NEAT.

FAQ

How many calories do I burn while sleeping?

You can burn around 50 to 100 calories per hour while sleeping, depending on your weight and sleeping position.

How many calories do I burn while sitting?

You can burn around 60 to 130 calories per hour while sitting, depending on your weight and the activity you are doing.

How many calories do I burn while standing?

You can burn around 80 to 150 calories per hour while standing, depending on your weight and the activity you are doing.

Pros and Cons

While it may be tempting to focus solely on calorie burn, it's important to remember that your overall health is more important than a number on a scale. While increasing your calorie burn can help with weight loss and weight maintenance, it's also important to focus on your nutrition, sleep, stress management, and overall physical activity. Additionally, some individuals may have a naturally lower BMR, so focusing solely on calorie burn may not be the most effective approach for them.

Overall, understanding how many calories you burn each day without exercise can be helpful in achieving your health and wellness goals. By focusing on small lifestyle changes that increase your NEAT and BMR, you can increase your overall calorie burn and improve your overall health.
