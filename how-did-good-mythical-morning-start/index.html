Introduction

Good Mythical Morning is a popular YouTube show hosted by Rhett McLaughlin and Link Neal. The duo has been making videos together since their college days in North Carolina, but it wasn't until 2012 that they launched their daily morning show that has now amassed over 16 million subscribers and 7.5 billion views. In this article, we'll take a closer look at how Good Mythical Morning started, its format, segments, guests, controversies, behind-the-scenes, spin-offs, impact, and answer some frequently asked questions.

The Beginning of Rhett and Link

Rhett and Link met in first grade and became best friends. They attended the same high school and later the same college, where they formed a comedy troupe called "I Love Local Commercials". After graduation, they moved to Los Angeles to pursue their dream of becoming entertainers. They landed various gigs, including writing and performing songs for TV commercials, but they still struggled to gain recognition.

It wasn't until they started making videos for YouTube in 2006 that they gained a cult following. Their quirky humor, musical talents, and unique brand of storytelling resonated with viewers. They created several popular series, such as "The Man Song", "Rub Some Bacon on It", and "Epic Rap Battle: Nerd vs. Geek". They also collaborated with other YouTubers, such as Smosh, Freddie Wong, and Hannah Hart.

The Birth of Good Mythical Morning

In January 2012, Rhett and Link launched Good Mythical Morning as a daily morning talk show. The idea was to have a show where they could do whatever they wanted, be themselves, and interact with their fans. The show quickly gained traction, thanks to its unique blend of humor, games, challenges, experiments, and food reviews. The show's tagline, "Good Mythical Morning - the show that's everything but a morning show", reflected its irreverent and unpredictable nature.

The show's initial format was simple. Rhett and Link would sit at a table and talk about random topics, such as weird news stories, pop culture trends, and personal anecdotes. They would also do taste tests, try weird foods, and play games. The show's early episodes were shot in their studio, which was a converted garage behind their homes in North Carolina. The show's production quality was modest, but it didn't matter because the content was fresh, funny, and authentic.

Format and Segments

Over the years, the format of Good Mythical Morning has evolved, but it still maintains its core elements of humor, games, and food. The show now has a more structured and polished format, with pre-recorded segments, live skits, and guest appearances. The show's segments include:

  • "Will It?" - where Rhett and Link try to make unusual food combinations work, such as "Will It Pizza?" or "Will It Cereal?"
  • "Taste Test" - where Rhett and Link try different types of food from around the world, such as "100-Year-Old Egg" or "Surströmming".
  • "Rank King" - where Rhett and Link rank things based on a specific theme, such as "Fast Food Fries" or "Disney Villains".
  • "Good Mythical Crew" - where the show's crew members play games and challenges.
  • "Wheel of Mythicality" - where Rhett and Link spin a wheel to determine what they'll do next, such as singing a song, doing a dance, or answering a question.

Guests, Collaborations, and Controversies

Good Mythical Morning has had numerous guests over the years, ranging from fellow YouTubers, to celebrities, to experts in various fields. Some notable guests include PewDiePie, Kevin Hart, Mark Hamill, and Bill Nye. Rhett and Link have also collaborated with other YouTube channels, such as Smosh, The Slow Mo Guys, and The Gregory Brothers. The show has also had its fair share of controversies, such as accusations of plagiarism, animal cruelty, and insensitivity towards certain communities. However, Rhett and Link have always addressed these controversies with transparency and humility, and have apologized when necessary.

Behind the Scenes

Good Mythical Morning has a dedicated team of writers, producers, editors, and crew members who help make the show possible. Rhett and Link are also involved in the creative process, coming up with ideas and scripts for the show. The show's production quality has significantly improved over the years, thanks to the resources and technology available to them. The show is now shot in a studio in Los Angeles, and the crew uses high-quality cameras, lighting, and sound equipment. Rhett and Link also have a podcast called "Ear Biscuits", where they talk about their personal lives, creative process, and behind-the-scenes stories of Good Mythical Morning.

Good Mythical More and Other Spin-Offs

In addition to Good Mythical Morning, Rhett and Link have also created several spin-off shows, such as "Good Mythical More", "Let's Talk About That", and "Mythical Kitchen". Good Mythical More is a supplementary show that airs after Good Mythical Morning, where Rhett and Link continue their conversations, play more games, and interact with their fans. Let's Talk About That is a talk show where Rhett and Link invite guests to discuss current events and pop culture. Mythical Kitchen is a cooking show where the crew experiments with bizarre and unconventional recipes.

The Impact of Good Mythical Morning

Good Mythical Morning has had a significant impact on YouTube and pop culture in general. The show has inspired other creators to make morning shows, talk shows, and game shows on YouTube. The show has also launched merchandise, such as t-shirts, mugs, and hats, and has gone on tours, performing live shows for their fans. Rhett and Link have become household names, and their humor and personalities have made them role models for their fans. The show has also won several awards, including a Streamy Award for Best Comedy Show and a Shorty Award for Best Web Series.
