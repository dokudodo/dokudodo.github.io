Things To Do In The Daytime In Las Vegas

Written by Bobby Feb 09, 2023 · 4 min read

.

Las Vegas is known for its vibrant nightlife, but there are plenty of things to do during the day. Whether you're looking for family-friendly activities or a relaxing day by the pool, Las Vegas has something for everyone. In this article, we'll cover some of the best things to do in the daytime in Las Vegas.

Visit the Hoover Dam

One of the most popular things to do in the daytime in Las Vegas is to visit the Hoover Dam. This engineering marvel spans the Colorado River on the border between Arizona and Nevada. Visitors can take a guided tour of the dam and learn about its history and construction. The Hoover Dam is about a 45-minute drive from Las Vegas and is open year-round.

Explore the Red Rock Canyon

If you're looking for a break from the hustle and bustle of the Strip, head to the Red Rock Canyon. This conservation area is located just a few miles west of Las Vegas and offers hiking trails, scenic drives, and stunning views of the Mojave Desert. The Red Rock Canyon is open daily and admission is $15 per vehicle.

Relax by the Pool

Las Vegas is known for its luxurious pool complexes, and many hotels offer day passes to non-guests. Spend a day lounging by the pool, sipping on a cocktail, and soaking up the sun. Some popular pool complexes include the Mandalay Bay Beach, the Venetian Pool Deck, and the Wynn Pool.

Take a Helicopter Tour of the Strip

See the bright lights of the Strip from above with a helicopter tour. Many companies offer daytime tours that take you over the iconic hotels and attractions of Las Vegas. This is a great way to get a bird's eye view of the city and see all the sights in a short amount of time.

Visit the Neon Museum

The Neon Museum is a unique museum that showcases the neon signs of Las Vegas's past. Visitors can take a guided tour of the museum and learn about the history of the signs and their significance to the city. The Neon Museum is open daily and tickets start at $19 for adults.

Check out the Bellagio Conservatory and Botanical Gardens

The Bellagio Conservatory and Botanical Gardens is a beautiful indoor garden located inside the Bellagio hotel. The garden features seasonal displays of flowers, plants, and trees, and is a peaceful oasis away from the crowds of the Strip. Admission to the garden is free and it's open 24 hours a day.

Visit the Shark Reef Aquarium

The Shark Reef Aquarium at Mandalay Bay is one of the largest aquariums in North America. Visitors can see over 2,000 animals, including sharks, rays, and sea turtles. There are also interactive exhibits and a touch pool where you can get up close and personal with some of the sea creatures. The aquarium is open daily and tickets start at $25 for adults.

Take a Stroll Down the Strip

One of the best things to do in the daytime in Las Vegas is to take a leisurely stroll down the Strip. See the iconic hotels and attractions up close, and take in the energy and excitement of the city. Be sure to bring comfortable shoes and plenty of sunscreen!

Go Shopping at the Forum Shops at Caesars Palace

The Forum Shops at Caesars Palace is one of the premier shopping destinations in Las Vegas. This luxurious shopping mall features high-end retailers like Gucci, Louis Vuitton, and Versace, as well as more affordable options like H&M and Zara. There are also several restaurants and cafes to choose from.

Visit the High Roller Observation Wheel

Take a ride on the High Roller Observation Wheel for stunning views of the Strip and the surrounding desert. The wheel stands 550 feet tall and is the tallest observation wheel in the world. Each cabin can hold up to 40 passengers, and rides last about 30 minutes. Tickets start at $25 for daytime rides.

Tips for Visiting Las Vegas

- Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated in the desert heat. - Wear comfortable shoes for walking around the city. - Check the weather forecast before planning outdoor activities. - Plan ahead and book tickets for popular attractions in advance. - Be aware of the dress codes for restaurants and nightclubs.

Conclusion

Las Vegas may be known for its nightlife, but there are plenty of things to do in the daytime as well. From exploring the natural wonders of Red Rock Canyon to shopping at high-end boutiques, there's something for everyone in this vibrant city. Plan your daytime activities ahead of time, and don't forget to take breaks to relax and recharge.
