Description

If you are a Weight Watchers member, you might be wondering if you can earn points for exercise. The answer is yes! Weight Watchers has a system in place that allows you to earn FitPoints for physical activity. These FitPoints can be used to offset the points you consume through food, allowing you to stay on track with your weight loss goals.

How to

The process of earning FitPoints for exercise is simple. All you need to do is track your physical activity using the Weight Watchers app or website. The app has a built-in activity tracker that allows you to log the type of activity you did, the duration, and the intensity level. Based on this information, the app will calculate the number of FitPoints you earned.

Step by step

  1. Open the Weight Watchers app or website
  2. Select "Track" from the bottom menu
  3. Select "Activity" from the top menu
  4. Select the type of activity you did
  5. Enter the duration and intensity level of the activity
  6. Submit your entry
  7. The app will calculate the number of FitPoints you earned

Tips

To maximize the number of FitPoints you earn, try to incorporate physical activity into your daily routine. This can include taking the stairs instead of the elevator, going for a walk during your lunch break, or doing a quick workout at home. You can also try different types of activities to keep things interesting and challenging.

Solution

Earning FitPoints for exercise is a great solution for Weight Watchers members who want to stay active and healthy while still enjoying their favorite foods. By tracking your physical activity and earning FitPoints, you can maintain a healthy balance between eating and exercise.

FAQ

Q: How many FitPoints can I earn for exercise?

A: The number of FitPoints you can earn for exercise depends on the type, duration, and intensity level of the activity. Higher intensity activities will earn you more FitPoints than lower intensity activities.

Q: Can I earn FitPoints for activities other than exercise?

A: Yes! You can earn FitPoints for any physical activity, including household chores, gardening, and even playing with your kids or pets.

Q: Do FitPoints expire?

A: Yes, FitPoints expire at the end of each week. This encourages you to stay active and earn more FitPoints each week.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Earn FitPoints for physical activity
  • Offset the points you consume through food
  • Maintain a healthy balance between eating and exercise

Cons:

  • FitPoints expire at the end of each week
  • You need to track your physical activity to earn FitPoints
  • Not all types of physical activity earn the same number of FitPoints
