What is UTI?

Before we dive into the length of classes at UTI, let's first define what UTI is. UTI or Universal Technical Institute is a vocational school that offers programs in automotive technology, diesel technology, collision repair and refinishing, and welding technology. UTI has campuses across the United States and is known for providing hands-on training to students in the automotive and transportation industries.

How Long Are Classes at UTI?

At UTI, the length of classes varies depending on the program you choose. Generally, classes at UTI are designed to provide students with hands-on training to prepare them for careers in the automotive and transportation industries. Here is a breakdown of the typical class duration for each program:

Automotive Technology

The Automotive Technology program at UTI is designed to be completed in 51 weeks. During this time, students will receive hands-on training in automotive technology, including engine repair, brake systems, and electrical systems. Classes are typically held Monday through Friday from 8:00 am to 2:30 pm.

Diesel Technology

The Diesel Technology program at UTI is designed to be completed in 45 weeks. During this time, students will receive hands-on training in diesel technology, including engine repair, fuel systems, and electrical systems. Classes are typically held Monday through Friday from 8:00 am to 2:30 pm.

Collision Repair and Refinishing

The Collision Repair and Refinishing program at UTI is designed to be completed in 51 weeks. During this time, students will receive hands-on training in collision repair and refinishing, including frame repair, welding, and painting. Classes are typically held Monday through Friday from 8:00 am to 2:30 pm.

Welding Technology

The Welding Technology program at UTI is designed to be completed in 36 weeks. During this time, students will receive hands-on training in welding technology, including MIG, TIG, and Stick welding. Classes are typically held Monday through Friday from 8:00 am to 2:30 pm.
