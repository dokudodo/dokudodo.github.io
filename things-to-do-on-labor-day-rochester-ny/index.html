Things To Do On Labor Day In Rochester Ny

If you're looking for a fun way to spend Labor Day weekend in Rochester, NY, you're in luck! There are plenty of activities and events happening around town. From music festivals to outdoor adventures, there's something for everyone. Here are our top picks for things to do on Labor Day in Rochester NY.

1. Attend the Rochester Labor Day Parade

The Rochester Labor Day Parade is an annual tradition that celebrates the achievements of labor and union workers. The parade features marching bands, floats, and community groups. It's a great way to show your support for the local labor movement while enjoying a festive atmosphere.

2. Visit the Rochester Public Market

The Rochester Public Market is open on Labor Day and offers a variety of fresh produce, meats, cheeses, and baked goods. You can also enjoy live music and food trucks. It's a great place to spend a few hours browsing and sampling local fare.

3. Explore the Genesee Riverway Trail

The Genesee Riverway Trail is a scenic trail that follows the Genesee River through downtown Rochester. You can walk, bike, or jog along the trail and enjoy views of the river and city skyline. It's a great way to get some exercise and enjoy the outdoors.

4. Attend the Rochester Fringe Festival

The Rochester Fringe Festival is a 10-day event that celebrates the arts. It features theater, dance, music, and comedy performances. There are also food trucks and vendors selling crafts and merchandise. It's a great way to experience the creative side of Rochester.

5. Visit the George Eastman Museum

The George Eastman Museum is a world-renowned museum that celebrates the history of photography and cinema. It's located in the former home of George Eastman, the founder of Kodak. You can explore the museum's galleries and gardens and learn about the history of photography and film.

6. Attend the Rochester Jazz Festival

The Rochester Jazz Festival is a multi-day event that features jazz performances by local and national artists. The festival takes place in various venues around downtown Rochester. It's a great way to enjoy some live music and experience the city's vibrant cultural scene.

7. Visit the Strong National Museum of Play

The Strong National Museum of Play is a family-friendly museum that celebrates the history of play. It features interactive exhibits and activities for kids and adults. You can explore the museum's collections of toys, games, and dolls and learn about the importance of play in our lives.

8. Take a Canal Boat Tour

The Erie Canal runs through Rochester, and you can take a canal boat tour to explore its history and significance. The tours usually last about an hour and take you through the locks and under the bridges of the canal. It's a great way to learn about the canal's role in Rochester's development.

9. Attend the Clothesline Festival

The Clothesline Festival is an annual event that showcases the work of local artists and craftspeople. It features booths selling jewelry, pottery, paintings, and other handmade items. There are also food vendors and live music. It's a great way to support local artists and find unique gifts.

10. Visit Seabreeze Amusement Park

Seabreeze Amusement Park is a family-friendly amusement park that offers rides, games, and water attractions. It's a great place to spend a day with friends or family. You can ride roller coasters, splash in the wave pool, or play carnival games. It's a fun way to celebrate Labor Day.

These are just a few of the many things to do on Labor Day in Rochester, NY. Whether you're looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, there's something for everyone. So get out and explore all that Rochester has to offer!
