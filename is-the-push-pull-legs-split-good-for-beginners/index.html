Is The Push Pull Legs Split Good For Beginners

Written by April Dec 21, 2022 · 4 min read

If you're new to weightlifting, you may have heard about the push pull legs (PPL) split. This type of workout routine is popular among bodybuilders and fitness enthusiasts, but is it suitable for beginners? In this article, we'll take a closer look at the PPL split and whether it's a good choice for those who are just starting out.

Description

The push pull legs split is a type of workout routine that targets different muscle groups on different days. The “push” day focuses on exercises that involve pushing movements like bench press, shoulder press, and triceps pushdowns. The “pull” day, on the other hand, focuses on exercises that involve pulling movements like pull-ups, rows, and bicep curls. The “legs” day focuses on exercises that target the lower body, such as squats, deadlifts, and lunges.

How to

If you're interested in trying out the PPL split, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Determine your training frequency

Decide how many days per week you want to work out. The PPL split is typically done over a 3-day or 6-day cycle.

Step 2: Choose your exercises

Select exercises that target the muscle groups you want to work on. For example, if you want to focus on your chest, you might choose bench press, incline press, and flyes for your push day.

Step 3: Plan your workouts

Create a workout plan that includes your chosen exercises for each day of the split. Be sure to include warm-up sets and rest periods.

Step 4: Execute your plan

Follow your workout plan and execute each exercise with proper form and technique. Remember to challenge yourself, but don't overdo it.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind if you're considering the PPL split:

  • Start with a lighter weight and gradually increase as you become stronger.
  • Be sure to include rest days in your plan to allow your muscles to recover.
  • Don't neglect your diet – proper nutrition is essential for muscle growth and recovery.
  • Ask a personal trainer or experienced lifter for guidance if you're unsure about proper form or technique.

Solution

The PPL split can be a good choice for beginners who are looking for a structured workout routine that targets different muscle groups. By focusing on specific movements and allowing for adequate rest and recovery, this type of split can help you build strength and muscle mass over time.

FAQ

Q: Is the PPL split suitable for everyone?

A: The PPL split can be effective for most people, but it may not be the best choice for those with certain medical conditions or injuries. It's always best to consult with a doctor or physical therapist before starting a new workout routine.

Q: How long should I stick with the PPL split?

A: It's a good idea to stick with the PPL split for at least 6-8 weeks to see results. After that, you may want to switch to a different type of workout routine to challenge your muscles in new ways.

Q: Can I modify the PPL split to suit my needs?

A: Yes, you can modify the PPL split based on your fitness goals and level of experience. For example, you may want to add more exercises or increase the weight as you become stronger.

Pros and Cons

Here are some potential pros and cons of the PPL split:

Pros:

  • Targets different muscle groups for a well-rounded workout
  • Allows for adequate rest and recovery between workouts
  • Can be modified to suit different fitness levels and goals

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for those with certain medical conditions or injuries
  • Requires some planning and preparation to execute properly
  • May become repetitive or boring over time

In conclusion, the PPL split can be a good choice for beginners who are looking for a structured workout routine that targets different muscle groups. With proper planning, execution, and modification, this type of split can help you build strength and muscle mass over time. However, it's important to listen to your body and consult with a professional if you experience any pain or discomfort during your workouts.
