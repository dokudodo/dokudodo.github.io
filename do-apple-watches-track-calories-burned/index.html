If you're looking to get fit or stay in shape, you may be wondering if your Apple Watch can track the calories you've burned during your workouts. The good news is that yes, Apple Watches do track the calories you've burned throughout the day. In this article, we will discuss how Apple Watches track calories burned and how to use this information to achieve your fitness goals.

How to Track Calories Burned on an Apple Watch

Tracking your calories burned on an Apple Watch is simple. All you need to do is open the "Activity" app on your watch and start a workout. Your watch will automatically track your heart rate and movement to calculate the number of calories you've burned.

Additionally, you can view your daily calorie burn by opening the "Activity" app on your iPhone. This app will show you a breakdown of the calories you've burned throughout the day, including during workouts and daily activities.

Step by Step Guide to Tracking Calories Burned on an Apple Watch

Below are the step-by-step instructions to track your calories burned on your Apple Watch:

  1. Open the "Activity" app on your Apple Watch.
  2. Select the type of workout you will be doing (e.g., running, cycling, swimming).
  3. Start your workout by tapping the "Start" button.
  4. Your watch will automatically track your heart rate and movement to calculate the number of calories you burn during your workout.
  5. When you're finished, swipe right to end your workout.
  6. Your watch will show you a summary of your workout, including the number of calories you burned.

Tips for Accurately Tracking Calories Burned on an Apple Watch

While Apple Watches are designed to track your calories burned accurately, there are a few things you can do to ensure your watch is accurately tracking your calories:

  • Make sure your watch is snug on your wrist to ensure accurate heart rate tracking.
  • Calibrate your watch to your stride length for more accurate distance tracking during outdoor runs or walks.
  • Make sure your watch is fully charged before starting a workout.
  • Update your personal information, including height, weight, and age, in the "Health" app on your iPhone for more accurate calorie tracking.

Solutions for Inaccurate Calorie Tracking on an Apple Watch

If you suspect your Apple Watch is not accurately tracking your calories burned, there are a few things you can do to troubleshoot the issue:

  • Make sure your watch is snug on your wrist to ensure accurate heart rate tracking.
  • Restart your watch to ensure it is functioning correctly.
  • Calibrate your watch to your stride length for more accurate distance tracking during outdoor runs or walks.
  • Update your personal information, including height, weight, and age, in the "Health" app on your iPhone for more accurate calorie tracking.

FAQs about Tracking Calories Burned on an Apple Watch

Q: Can I track calories burned without starting a workout?

A: Yes, your Apple Watch tracks your daily calorie burn even if you do not start a workout.

Q: How accurate is the calorie tracking on an Apple Watch?

A: The calorie tracking on an Apple Watch is generally accurate, but it can vary depending on factors like heart rate variability and individual metabolism.

Q: Can I track calories burned during swimming?

A: Yes, you can track calories burned during swimming by selecting the "Pool Swim" workout option and starting your workout.

Pros and Cons of Using an Apple Watch to Track Calories Burned

Pros:

  • Accurately tracks your daily calorie burn.
  • Easy to use.
  • Provides motivation to reach your fitness goals.
  • Allows you to track your progress over time.

Cons:

  • May not be as accurate as a heart rate monitor.
  • Requires an Apple Watch and iPhone.
  • May not be suitable for all types of workouts.

In conclusion, if you're looking to track your calories burned during workouts or daily activities, an Apple Watch is a great tool to use. With its accurate tracking and easy-to-use interface, an Apple Watch can help you achieve your fitness goals and stay motivated along the way.
